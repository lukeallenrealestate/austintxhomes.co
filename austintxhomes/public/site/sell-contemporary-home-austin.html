The Approach

How Luke Markets

01 — Competitive Analysis
Beating New Construction

We start by mapping your home against the actual new construction your buyers are comparing. What's the finish-level gap? What do you have that a builder can't offer — lot character, mature landscaping, established neighborhood, custom architecture? This analysis drives the entire pricing and marketing strategy.

02 — Photography
Architecture, Space & Light

Contemporary photography is wide, clean, and architectural — it captures volume, sightlines, and the relationship between indoor and outdoor space. Luke works with photographers who understand how to shoot glass walls, polished concrete, and open-concept plans in a way that makes the space feel lived-in and aspirational simultaneously.

03 — Finish Inventory
Cataloging What You Have

Before we price or market, we document every finish upgrade — appliance brands and models, countertop material and source, flooring specs, window manufacturer, smart home systems, solar system size and remaining warranty, EV charger amperage. This inventory drives the listing copy and justifies the price against comparable homes with lesser finishes.

04 — Buyer Targeting
The Tech & Design Buyer

Contemporary Austin buyers are disproportionately tech and finance professionals — many relocating from San Francisco, Seattle, or New York with high income and strong aesthetic standards. Targeted digital campaigns by income, profession, and relocation status put your listing in front of buyers who are primed to pay for what you have — before they've committed to a builder.

05 — Pricing
Finish-Level Comparables

Comps that match your finish quality only. A similar square footage with spec-builder finishes is not a comparable for your home with Sub-Zero and custom cabinetry. Luke adjusts for appliance value, countertop quality, flooring grade, smart home investment, outdoor living, and lot premium — producing a defensible price that buyers respect rather than challenge.

06 — Timing
When to List

Contemporary buyers are active year-round — they're not seasonal the way family buyers are. But they do respond to inventory cycles. Luke monitors active contemporary and new construction inventory in your price range weekly, so we can time your launch to minimize direct competition and maximize showing activity in the first seven days.

Questions

Frequently Asked
Questions

How does my contemporary home compete with new construction in Austin? +
The key is differentiation — and it works in your favor more often than sellers expect. Your home is on an established lot with mature landscaping, a known neighborhood, and no builder warranty uncertainty. If your finishes are high-quality and your systems are maintained, buyers comparing you to new construction are trading spec-builder materials for something custom and finished. The agent's job is to make that comparison explicitly favorable.
Do smart home features and solar add real value when selling? +
Smart home systems add value when they're documented and transferable — buyers won't pay for tech they can't use. Solar panels add meaningful value in Austin (typically $3–5 per watt depending on system size, ownership vs. lease, and remaining warranty). EV chargers are increasingly expected above $700K. The key is having a clear inventory of every system, its age, condition, and a simple handoff process. Buyers who want these features will pay; those who don't won't object if the systems are well-documented.
What finishes matter most to contemporary buyers in Austin? +
In order of buyer impact: (1) Kitchen — appliance brand, countertop material, and cabinet quality; (2) Primary bath — fixtures and tile quality; (3) Flooring — wide-plank hardwood, polished concrete, or large-format tile; (4) Windows and doors — Andersen 400 or better, steel frame windows, and solid-core entry doors; (5) Outdoor living — covered patio, pool, or outdoor kitchen. Generic builder finishes in a contemporary shell are a liability; high-end finishes are the asset.
Who typically buys contemporary homes in Austin? +
Contemporary Austin buyers are typically tech and finance professionals — many relocating from coastal cities — who have strong aesthetic preferences and are accustomed to higher finish standards. They research before touring, care deeply about design coherence, and respond to listing photography that captures the architecture. They also include design-forward families upgrading from starter homes and empty nesters downsizing into easier-to-maintain modern spaces. Remote work has significantly expanded this buyer pool.
How do you accurately price a contemporary home in Austin? +
Pricing a contemporary home requires comps that match on finish quality — not just square footage and neighborhood. A comparable home with builder-grade finishes vs. your Wolf appliances and imported stone countertops can represent a $150–300K difference in value that square-footage-based pricing misses entirely. I assess your finish level against current competing inventory, adjust for lot premium, architecture quality, smart home systems, and outdoor living — then benchmark against sales where buyers specifically paid for what you have.
