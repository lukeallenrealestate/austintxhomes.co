Why a CMA Beats Zillow

Algorithms Don't Know
Your Neighborhood

Zillow's Zestimate is calculated from public tax records, listing data, and statistical models. It has no idea about the custom kitchen you renovated last year, the fact that your lot backs to a greenbelt, or that two competing homes in your price range just went under contract.

A Comparative Market Analysis looks at the specific comparable sales that buyers and their agents will use when making offers on your home — adjusted for condition, location, and timing. It's the same methodology your buyer's agent will use to write their offer, so you should know the number before they do.

In a market like Austin, where neighborhoods vary enormously in value and buyer demand shifts quarterly, the difference between a Zestimate and reality can be $50,000 or more.

How accurate is a CMA compared to Zillow? +
A Comparative Market Analysis is significantly more accurate than automated valuations like Zillow's Zestimate. A CMA uses actual closed sales of comparable homes in your neighborhood, accounts for condition, upgrades, and micro-location factors that algorithms miss, and reflects current buyer demand in your specific area. Zillow's median error rate in Texas is around 5–8%, which on a $600,000 home is $30,000–$48,000. A CMA from a local agent who knows Austin's neighborhoods will be far tighter.

What factors affect my Austin home's value? +
The main factors are: location and neighborhood (school district matters enormously in Austin), square footage, lot size, age and condition, recent upgrades (kitchens, baths, primary suite), the number of competing active listings, and current buyer demand in your price range. Austin's market also varies significantly by submarket — Barton Hills vs. Pflugerville, for example, behave very differently. Architectural style also matters: mid-century modern homes in particular command an authenticity premium and attract a distinct buyer pool — see how MCM homes are valued and marketed differently.
When is the best time to sell a home in Austin? +
Spring (March–May) historically sees the most buyer activity in Austin, but the best time depends on your personal situation and current market conditions. With live MLS data updated every 15 minutes, Luke can tell you exactly how your neighborhood is performing right now — days on market, list-to-sale ratios, and price trends — so you can make an informed decision on timing.
Do I have to sell after getting the report? +
Absolutely not. Many homeowners request a CMA to track their equity, plan a future move, or evaluate a refinance. There is zero pressure and zero obligation. If and when you're ready to sell, Luke is happy to help — but the report is yours regardless.
