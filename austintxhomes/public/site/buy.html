Common Questions

Buyer FAQ

How much does it cost to use a buyer's agent?

As a buyer, you typically pay nothing out of pocket. The seller covers buyer agent compensation through the sale proceeds. You get full representation — search, negotiation, contracts, closing — at no direct cost to you. Learn more about working with a licensed Austin Realtor →

How long does it take to buy a home in Austin?

From active search to closing, most buyers take 60–120 days. It depends on your flexibility on neighborhoods, how quickly you get pre-approved, and market conditions. The closing process itself is typically 21–30 days once you're under contract.

Do I need to be pre-approved before touring homes?

Yes. Austin's market is competitive and moves fast. Sellers won't accept offers without a pre-approval letter, and some listings get 10+ showings the first weekend. I can connect you with lenders who get pre-approvals done in 24 hours.

Can I buy a home in Austin from out of state?

Absolutely. I work with remote buyers regularly. We do virtual tours via FaceTime, I walk every home on your behalf and give you my honest assessment, and you can sign all documents electronically.

What's the Austin market like right now?

Austin has shifted from its 2021–2022 peak but remains competitive in desirable neighborhoods. Inventory is up, which is good for buyers. Days on market have increased, giving buyers more time to evaluate. Interest rates matter more than ever — get pre-approved for the best strategy.

How do I know what a home is actually worth?

I run a comparative market analysis (CMA) on every home we consider seriously. This uses recent, comparable sales — not Zillow estimates — to determine true market value. You'll never go into an offer blind.
