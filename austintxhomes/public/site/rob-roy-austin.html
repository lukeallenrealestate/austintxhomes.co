About Rob Roy

Austin's Premier Gated Estate Address

Rob Roy occupies a singular position in the Austin luxury market. Guard-gated, deeply wooded, and just 10 miles from downtown — it delivers the privacy and scale of a true estate community without the hour-long commute that usually comes with it.

The community sits in the Westlake Hills corridor off Bee Cave Road, tucked into the cedar-covered limestone hills west of Loop 360. Lots range from one to three or more acres, heavily screened by mature trees, with homes positioned to capture views of either the Austin skyline to the east or the Hill Country to the west — sometimes both.

The architectural range is wide: original custom builds from the late 1980s and 90s sit alongside recent teardown-rebuilds and mid-century estates that have been extensively renovated. The rebuild market has been particularly active — Rob Roy lots command $1.5M–$3M before a single nail is driven, which gives you a sense of how the land alone is valued.

  • 24-hour guard-gated entry — staffed security year-round
  • Eanes ISD — home of Westlake High School, ranked top 1% in Texas
  • Custom estates on 1–3+ acre wooded lots
  • Hill Country and Austin skyline views from elevated positions
  • 10 miles from downtown via Loop 360 or Bee Cave Road
  • Near Lake Austin, Barton Creek, and the Barton Creek Greenbelt
  • Active teardown/custom rebuild market — new construction available
  • One of Austin's lowest-turnover communities — limited inventory
What Drives Value in Rob Roy

Six Reasons Rob Roy Commands Its Premium

01

Guard-Gated Security

Staffed 24-hour gate — not a keypad, not a code. One of only a handful of truly guard-gated communities in Austin. The security and exclusivity layer is a durable value premium with no equivalent substitute at this price point.

02

Eanes ISD

Westlake High School is one of the most coveted public schools in Texas. For families making a $5M+ purchase, the school district is often the primary filter — and Eanes ISD narrows the field to a very short list of Austin communities.

03

Downtown Proximity

Ten miles. Fifteen minutes on a good day. For executives and professionals who need to be downtown regularly, Rob Roy is the only guard-gated estate community that doesn't require a highway commute to use the city.

04

Lot Scale & Privacy

1–3+ acre lots with mature cedar and live oak canopy mean that even on a $3M entry-level purchase, you have genuine privacy. Homes aren't stacked. You don't see your neighbor's house from your pool. That's rare inside the Austin city limits.

05

Views

Elevated Hill Country terrain delivers two distinct view categories: downtown Austin skyline to the east (particularly dramatic at night), and rolling cedar Hill Country to the west and south. The best lots have both — and they command accordingly.

06

Scarcity

Rob Roy rarely has more than a handful of active listings at any given time. Owners stay. The combination of limited supply and consistent demand from the top tier of Austin buyers means the market here is historically more insulated from downturns than the broader luxury segment.

Market Overview

Rob Roy Price Tiers

The Rob Roy market breaks into four tiers based on condition, position, and view quality. Understanding where a given home sits within this structure is essential to buying or selling at the right price.

$3M – $4.5M
Entry-level Rob Roy — typically older custom homes in original or lightly updated condition on strong lots. Often the most compelling buy for investors planning a renovation or teardown/rebuild. Lot value alone justifies much of the purchase price.
$4.5M – $7M
The core of the market. Renovated or recently built custom homes, 4,500–7,000 sqft, with updated kitchens, primary suites, and outdoor living. This tier moves most frequently and represents the best liquidity in the community.
$7M – $10M
High-specification custom builds and extensively renovated estates. Typically 6,000–9,000 sqft with guest houses, pools, home theaters, and strong view orientation. Limited supply — often purchased off-market or within weeks of listing.
$10M – $15M+
Trophy properties — bespoke new construction, unobstructed dual-direction views, resort-level outdoor living, and full compound configurations with guest or staff quarters. These trade on reputation and relationships as much as MLS exposure.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked About Rob Roy

What is Rob Roy in Austin TX?

Rob Roy is a guard-gated luxury estate community in the Westlake Hills area of Austin, located in the 78746 ZIP code approximately 10 miles west of downtown. Accessed via Rob Roy Road off Bee Cave Road near Loop 360, it is known for large custom estate homes on 1–3 acre wooded lots, staffed security, Eanes ISD schools, and panoramic views of the Austin skyline and Hill Country.

What are home prices in Rob Roy Austin?

Rob Roy home prices range from approximately $3M for entry-level properties to $15M+ for top-tier custom estates. The core of the market — renovated or recently built custom homes — runs $4.5M–$7M. High-specification builds with unobstructed views and premium outdoor living range $7M–$10M+. Lot values alone often exceed $1.5M–$3M, reflecting the scarcity of large wooded parcels this close to downtown Austin.

What school district serves Rob Roy Austin?

Rob Roy is served by Eanes Independent School District (Eanes ISD), which includes Westlake High School — consistently ranked among the top public high schools in Texas and the nation. For families purchasing in the $3M–$15M range, Eanes ISD is frequently the decisive factor when comparing Rob Roy to other Austin luxury addresses.

Is Rob Roy a gated community?

Yes — Rob Roy has staffed 24-hour guard gate entry. This is not a keypad-access or camera-monitored gate but a staffed security checkpoint with personnel on site around the clock. It is one of Austin's few genuinely guard-gated residential communities, and this security infrastructure is a durable premium in the community's valuation.

How close is Rob Roy to downtown Austin?

Rob Roy is approximately 10–12 miles west of downtown Austin, typically a 15–25 minute drive via Loop 360 or Bee Cave Road to MoPac. This proximity — combined with guard-gated privacy, estate lot scale, and Eanes ISD schools — is the core value proposition: the estate lifestyle without the distance penalty that most comparable Texas communities require.
