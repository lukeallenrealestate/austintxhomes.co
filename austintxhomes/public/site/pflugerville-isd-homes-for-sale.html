The School District

Why Families Choose
Pflugerville ISD

Pflugerville ISD is one of the fastest-growing school districts in the Austin metro — and for good reason. With over 30,000 students, strong magnet programs, and a diverse community that reflects the real makeup of Central Texas, it delivers solid academics at a price point that actually works for most families.

The district serves the city of Pflugerville, parts of northeast Austin, and portions of Manor. It sits directly in the path of Austin's tech boom — Samsung's semiconductor fab, Dell's Round Rock headquarters, and Tesla Gigafactory are all within a short drive. That proximity to major employers has fueled steady housing demand and new construction across the district.

Four high schools — Connally, Hendrickson, Weiss, and Pflugerville — give families options, and the district's magnet programs in STEM and fine arts attract students from across the area. Class sizes are manageable, extracurriculars are strong, and the community is engaged without the pressure-cooker intensity of some higher-priced districts.

For buyers, Pflugerville ISD represents the sweet spot: good schools, newer housing stock, and home prices that are 50–70% lower than what you'd pay in Eanes or Lake Travis ISD. The trade-off is less prestige on paper — but for the money, the education and quality of life are hard to beat anywhere in the Austin metro.

Market Reality

What Buying in Pflugerville ISD
Actually Looks Like

Pflugerville ISD is one of the best value plays in the Austin metro — and the market reflects it. Entry-level homes in the $275K–$375K range move quickly, especially move-in-ready properties in established neighborhoods like Blackhawk, Wells Branch, and Highland Park. If you're a first-time buyer, this is one of the strongest markets to target.

New construction from major builders has added significant inventory over the past few years, which means you're less likely to get into bidding wars compared to supply-constrained districts like Eanes or Lake Travis. Builders are actively competing for buyers in communities like Falcon Pointe, Avalon, and Sorento — and that competition works in your favor.

Above $400K, the market shifts. Days on market stretch, negotiation is more common, and you can often secure closing cost contributions or rate buydowns. This is where the premium newer construction lives — larger lots, upgraded finishes, and homes that would cost $600K+ in Round Rock or $1M+ in Westlake.

The investor angle is real too. Pflugerville's proximity to Samsung, Dell, and Tesla Gigafactory creates strong rental demand from tech workers who want to live close to work without paying Austin-core prices. Cap rates here are more favorable than most Austin submarkets.

Common Questions

Pflugerville ISD FAQ

What cities and neighborhoods are in Pflugerville ISD?
Pflugerville ISD serves the city of Pflugerville, parts of northeast Austin, and portions of Manor. Primary zip codes include 78660, 78753, 78754, and 78725. Neighborhoods include Blackhawk, Falcon Pointe, Avalon, Wells Branch, Sorento, Highland Park, Meadows of Blackhawk, and Villages of Hidden Lake.
What are the schools in Pflugerville ISD?
Pflugerville ISD has four high schools — Connally, Hendrickson, Weiss, and Pflugerville — along with middle schools including Dessau, Kelly Lane, Pflugerville, and Park Crest. Notable elementary schools include Brookhollow, Spring Hill, Windermere, Copperfield, and Pflugerville Elementary. The district serves over 30,000 students with strong magnet programs.
What is the typical home price range in Pflugerville ISD?
Homes in Pflugerville ISD typically range from $275K to $600K. Entry-level homes in Central Pflugerville and Highland Park start around $275K–$400K. Established communities like Blackhawk and Wells Branch run $300K–$475K. Newer master-planned neighborhoods like Falcon Pointe and Avalon are $350K–$500K. Premium construction in Sorento and Villages of Hidden Lake tops out around $400K–$600K.
Is Pflugerville ISD a good school district?
Pflugerville ISD is TEA B-rated and one of the largest suburban districts in Austin with over 30,000 students. It offers strong magnet programs, competitive athletics, and a diverse student body. While it doesn't carry the same prestige as Eanes or Lake Travis ISD, it delivers solid academics at a fraction of the housing cost — making it a top choice for families who prioritize value alongside quality education.
How competitive is the Pflugerville ISD real estate market?
The entry-level market ($275K–$375K) is competitive for move-in-ready homes. New construction from major builders provides additional inventory, reducing bidding wars. Above $400K, buyers have more negotiating power and can often secure closing cost contributions. The area also attracts investors due to strong rental demand from tech workers at Samsung, Dell, and Tesla Gigafactory.
Can I commute to downtown Austin or the tech corridor from Pflugerville?
Yes. Pflugerville is 20–30 minutes from downtown Austin via I-35 or SH 130 toll road, and just 15 minutes from the Domain tech corridor (Apple, Amazon, Meta, Indeed). Samsung's semiconductor fab is nearby in north Austin, Dell's HQ is minutes away in Round Rock, and Tesla Gigafactory is accessible via SH 130 — providing fast north-south travel that avoids I-35 congestion.
