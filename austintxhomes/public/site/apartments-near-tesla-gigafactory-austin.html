Know Before You Search

Gigafactory Texas Is in Southeast Austin

Most Austin apartment guides recommend the Domain, Round Rock, and Cedar Park — neighborhoods built around Apple, Dell, and Samsung campuses in north Austin. That's a 35-45 minute commute to Tesla. The neighborhoods below are the ones that actually work.

10–15 min

Del Valle / SE Austin

via SH-130 or TX-71

Closest to the factory. Newer apartment complexes, rapidly developing. Lower rents but fewer restaurants and walkable amenities. Best for people who prioritize short commute and saving money.

15–22 min

East Austin / E Riverside

via TX-71 W or Riverside Dr

The sweet spot. Walkable restaurants, bars, coffee shops, genuine neighborhood character. East 6th, East 7th, East Cesar Chavez corridors. Reverse commute to the factory avoids I-35 entirely. Most Tesla employees end up here.

18–25 min

Mueller / Windsor Park

via US-183 to SH-130

Master-planned community with parks, trails, restaurants, and a genuine neighborhood feel. Family-friendly. H-E-B, Alamo Drafthouse, and local shops within walking distance. Slightly longer commute but exceptional quality of life.

20–30 min

South Austin / Manchaca

via Slaughter Ln to SH-130

South Congress vibe without South Congress prices. Older, more character-driven neighborhoods. Good food scene along S 1st and Manchaca Rd. Commute uses SH-130 toll road (fast, uncongested).

25–35 min

Manor / Pflugerville

via US-290 or SH-130

Budget-friendly. Newer suburban complexes, $1,000-$1,400/mo for a 1BR. Trade-off: longer commute and less walkable. Best for people saving aggressively or who prefer newer construction at lower prices.

25–35 min

Kyle / Buda

via I-35 to SH-130

Small-town feel south of Austin. Rapidly growing with new apartment construction. Significantly cheaper rents than central Austin. Commute is manageable via SH-130 but I-35 section can slow down during peak hours.

Relocation Intel

Does Tesla Offer Relocation Assistance?

Tesla offers relocation packages that vary by role, level, and hire type. Here's what most Tesla Austin employees report:

Engineering & corporate roles — typically $5,000–$15,000 domestic relocation stipend covering moving expenses and temporary housing. Some roles include a home-finding trip reimbursement.

Production & manufacturing roles — relocation packages are less common for hourly production roles, but Tesla has offered signing bonuses and temporary housing assistance during hiring surges.

Senior / executive transfers — more comprehensive packages including lease-break reimbursement, spouse job search support, and extended temporary housing.

Key point: packages are negotiable. If you haven't received a relocation offer, ask during negotiation — especially for hard-to-fill roles. The cost of a $10K relocation package is trivial compared to re-opening a headcount.

Common Questions

Tesla Apartment FAQ

Does Tesla offer relocation assistance for Austin employees?+
Tesla offers relocation packages that vary by role and level. Most full-time engineering and production management roles include a relocation stipend, typically $5,000–$15,000+ for domestic moves. The package usually covers moving expenses and temporary housing but not ongoing rent. Senior roles may receive more comprehensive packages. Packages are negotiable — always ask during the offer process.
What are the best apartments near Tesla Gigafactory Austin?+
For the shortest commute (10-15 min), look along SH-130 and TX-71 in Del Valle and SE Austin. For a better lifestyle at slightly longer commute (20-25 min), East Austin along E Riverside and the East 7th corridor offers walkable restaurants and nightlife with a reverse commute to the factory. Mueller offers a family-friendly option at 20-25 min. Budget options in Manor and Pflugerville run $1,000-$1,400/mo for 1BR but add 25-35 minutes.
How much do apartments cost near Tesla Gigafactory?+
Del Valle / SE Austin (10-15 min): $1,200–$1,800/mo for 1BR. East Austin (20 min): $1,400–$2,200/mo. Mueller (22 min): $1,500–$2,000/mo. Budget suburbs (Manor, Pflugerville, Kyle): $1,000–$1,400/mo. The sweet spot for most Tesla employees is the East Austin / SE Austin corridor — reasonable rent, genuine neighborhood, and a reverse commute that avoids I-35.
Where is Tesla Gigafactory Austin located?+
Tesla Gigafactory Texas (Giga Texas) is at 13101 Harold Green Road, Del Valle, TX 78617 — southeast Travis County, near SH-130 and TX-71, adjacent to Austin-Bergstrom International Airport and Circuit of the Americas. This is the opposite direction from Austin's north-side tech corridor (Apple, Dell, Samsung), so traditional tech-worker neighborhoods like Cedar Park add 35-45 minutes to your commute.
What is Tesla's location strategy for Gigafactory Austin?+
Tesla chose southeast Austin for airport proximity (supply chain logistics), SH-130 access (uncongested toll road), cheap industrial land (2,500+ acre campus), Colorado River water access (battery manufacturing), and Texas's business-friendly environment. For employees, this means SE Austin and East Austin offer the best commutes, while NW Austin neighborhoods are poorly positioned.
Should I rent or buy near Tesla Gigafactory?+
Rent first — especially if you're new to Austin. A 12-month lease gives you time to learn the city, understand commute patterns, and figure out which neighborhood fits your lifestyle. Most Tesla employees who rent for a year before buying end up choosing a different neighborhood than they would have picked sight-unseen.
