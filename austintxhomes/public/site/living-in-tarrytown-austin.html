Life in Tarrytown

What It's Like to Live in Tarrytown

Morning in Tarrytown starts the way it has for decades: coffee on the front porch, watching the light filter through a canopy of live oaks that arch completely over the street. The air is still cool — this side of Austin catches the breeze off Lake Austin — and the only sound is a neighbor's screen door closing as they head out for a walk. If you have kids at Casis Elementary, the walk to school takes eight minutes on quiet sidewalks lined with mid-century ranch homes and the occasional stone cottage from the 1930s. There are no stoplights between most Tarrytown homes and the Casis front door. Luke Allen has walked this route with dozens of families who moved to Tarrytown specifically for this school, and the walk alone is usually what seals the decision.

Tarrytown's daily rhythm is unhurried in a way that feels almost impossible this close to a major downtown. By afternoon, kids are swimming at Deep Eddy Pool — Austin's oldest swimming pool, spring-fed, and free for Austin residents — while their parents read on the stone bleachers. Reed Park fills up with basketball games and dogs chasing tennis balls. The pocket parks scattered throughout Tarrytown give the neighborhood a green, spacious feeling despite the relatively modest lot sizes. Luke Allen often tells clients that Tarrytown doesn't feel like a city neighborhood — it feels like a small town that happens to be five minutes from the office towers on Congress Avenue.

The commute is one of Tarrytown's most underrated advantages. MoPac (Loop 1) runs along the western edge of the neighborhood, putting downtown Austin about 10 minutes away in normal traffic. The Domain and North Austin tech corridor are 15 minutes up MoPac. Luke Allen works with buyers relocating from cities like San Francisco and Chicago who are stunned that a neighborhood this close to downtown can feel this quiet. The secret is that Tarrytown has almost no commercial development within its interior — there are no bars, no through-traffic shortcuts, no late-night noise. The commercial life of the neighborhood exists at its edges: Lake Austin Blvd to the south (restaurants, coffee, the boat launch) and Exposition Blvd to the east (the Tarrytown Shopping Center, Tacodeli, neighborhood services). Inside, it's just houses, trees, and families.

What makes Tarrytown genuinely special — and what Luke Allen hears from long-time residents more than anything else — is the community. This is a neighborhood where people stay. Families who bought here when their kids started at Casis are still here twenty years later, now watching their grandchildren walk the same streets. Neighbors know each other by name. The Tarrytown Fourth of July parade is a genuine neighborhood tradition, not a city event. If you're moving to Austin and looking for a place with roots, character, and a real sense of belonging — not just a nice house in a good location — Tarrytown is the neighborhood Luke Allen recommends first. Schools

Schools in Tarrytown

Austin ISD · Elementary

Casis Elementary

Consistently rated one of Austin's best elementary schools and the number one reason families choose Tarrytown. Casis is walkable from most of the neighborhood, has a deeply engaged parent community, and produces some of the strongest test scores in Austin ISD. Luke Allen considers Casis the single most important factor in Tarrytown's long-term property values — homes zoned to Casis hold value better than almost any other elementary zone in Austin.

Austin ISD · Middle School

O. Henry Middle School

Feeds from Casis Elementary and serves grades 6-8. O. Henry has a strong academic reputation, solid extracurricular programs, and benefits from the same engaged parent base that supports Casis. Located in the Bouldin Creek area, it is accessible from Tarrytown via a short drive down MoPac.

Austin ISD · High School

Austin High School

Situated on the southern edge of Tarrytown along Lady Bird Lake, Austin High is walkable for many Tarrytown students. The campus is one of the most scenic in the state, with direct trail access to Lady Bird Lake. Austin High offers strong AP programs, competitive athletics, and a diverse student body that reflects the broader west Austin community.

Eanes ISD · Select Addresses

Eanes ISD / Westlake High School

Portions of western Tarrytown near the Westlake border feed into Eanes ISD, which includes the highly ranked Westlake High School. Eanes ISD is consistently rated among the top school districts in Texas. This boundary line runs through Tarrytown, meaning some homes are in Austin ISD and some are in Eanes ISD — the difference can be a single block.

Important: School boundaries in Tarrytown vary by exact address, and the Austin ISD / Eanes ISD boundary line runs through the neighborhood. Things to Do in Tarrytown

🏊

Deep Eddy Pool

Austin's oldest swimming pool — spring-fed, open year-round, and free for Austin residents. A Tarrytown institution since 1915. Lap swimming in the morning, families in the afternoon, and the best sunset spot in the neighborhood.

🚶

Hike & Bike Trail

Lady Bird Lake trail access from the south end of Tarrytown. Run, bike, or walk the 10-mile loop along the lake. Direct access from Austin High and the Deep Eddy area without needing to drive anywhere.

⚽

Reed Park

The neighborhood park at the heart of Tarrytown. Playground, basketball courts, tennis courts, and open green space. The kind of park where you run into people you know every single visit.

🛶

Walsh Boat Landing

Kayak and paddleboard launch on Lake Austin. One of the few public access points to the lake, and it's right at the bottom of the neighborhood. Weekend mornings on the water are a Tarrytown ritual.

🛍

Tarrytown Shopping Center

The neighborhood's small commercial hub on Exposition Blvd. Local shops, services, and the kind of businesses that have been here for years. Walkable from most of the neighborhood.

☕

Lake Austin Blvd

The southern edge of Tarrytown runs along Lake Austin Blvd — home to restaurants, coffee shops, and local businesses. Hula Hut, Cafe Josie, and Mozart's Coffee are all within a short walk or bike ride.

🦚

Mayfield Park & Preserve

A hidden gem on the northwest edge of Tarrytown. Free-roaming peacocks, hiking trails through the preserve, and beautiful cottage gardens. One of Austin's best-kept secrets, and it's in Tarrytown's backyard.

🚗

MoPac Access

MoPac (Loop 1) runs along the western edge of Tarrytown, putting downtown Austin 10 minutes away and the Domain/North Austin tech corridor 15 minutes out. The commute is a major reason Luke Allen recommends Tarrytown to professionals.

Luke Allen's Tarrytown Picks

Restaurants & Coffee near Tarrytown

Cafe Josie

Lake Austin Blvd

Farm-to-table dining in a warm, intimate setting. Cafe Josie has been a Tarrytown date-night staple for years. Seasonal menus, excellent wine list, and the kind of place where Luke Allen takes out-of-town clients who want to experience the neighborhood.

Juliet Italian Kitchen

Lake Austin Blvd

Casual Italian with a great patio overlooking the lake. Handmade pasta, wood-fired pizza, and the best happy hour in the Lake Austin Blvd corridor. Family-friendly and walkable from the southern end of Tarrytown.

Hula Hut

Lake Austin Blvd

Tex-Mex meets Hawaiian on the shores of Lake Austin. The waterfront patio is legendary. It's touristy, yes — but Tarrytown residents still go regularly because the location and sunset views are unbeatable.

Tacodeli

Exposition Blvd

Austin's favorite taco shop, and the Tarrytown location on Exposition is the most neighborhood-y of all their spots. The Otto is Luke Allen's go-to order. Breakfast tacos here are a genuine Tarrytown morning ritual.

Sweetish Hill Bakery

West 6th Street

A West Austin institution — pastries, bread, sandwiches, and some of the best coffee in the area. Just east of Tarrytown on West 6th, Sweetish Hill has been a morning stop for Tarrytown residents for decades.

Mozart's Coffee

Lake Austin Blvd

Lakeside coffee roastery with a massive multi-level deck overlooking Lake Austin. Best known for their holiday light show, but Tarrytown locals come year-round for the espresso and the views. Luke Allen's top pick for a weekend morning coffee spot.

Frequently Asked Questions

Living in Tarrytown — FAQ

Is Tarrytown a good neighborhood in Austin?

Yes. Tarrytown is consistently ranked as one of Austin's most desirable neighborhoods. It offers walkable access to Casis Elementary (rated 10/10), a 5-minute commute to downtown via MoPac, mature live oak canopy, and a quiet residential character that's rare for an inner-city location. Luke Allen has helped dozens of families find homes in Tarrytown and considers it one of Austin's strongest long-term investments.

What school district is Tarrytown Austin in?

Most of Tarrytown is in Austin ISD (Casis Elementary, O. Henry Middle, Austin High). However, portions of western Tarrytown near the Westlake border feed into Eanes ISD, which includes Westlake High School. The boundary runs through the neighborhood block by block. Luke Allen verifies school zoning for every property — this is critical for families.

How far is Tarrytown from downtown Austin?

Tarrytown is approximately 2 miles from downtown Austin. Via MoPac, most Tarrytown residents reach downtown in 5-10 minutes during normal traffic. Even during rush hour, the commute rarely exceeds 15 minutes. Luke Allen regularly works with professionals who chose Tarrytown specifically for this commute advantage.

Is Tarrytown walkable?

Tarrytown has a Walk Score of 65+, which is above average for Austin. Most families walk to Casis Elementary, Deep Eddy Pool, Reed Park, and the Tarrytown Shopping Center on Exposition. Lake Austin Blvd offers walkable restaurants and coffee. Luke Allen notes that while a car is helpful for grocery runs, daily life in Tarrytown can be surprisingly walkable.

What is the vibe of Tarrytown?

Established, family-oriented, and quietly upscale. Live oaks arching over wide streets, kids riding bikes to the pool, neighbors who know each other by name. It's not trendy or flashy — it's the kind of neighborhood where families stay for decades. Luke Allen describes Tarrytown as "the closest thing Austin has to a New England village feel — but with sunshine."

Are there apartments or rentals in Tarrytown?

Tarrytown is predominantly single-family homes, and rental inventory is limited. You may find a handful of duplexes, garage apartments, or occasional homes for rent, but there are no large apartment complexes. Rentals typically start around $2,500/month for smaller units. Luke Allen can help identify rental opportunities as they come to market.

Is Tarrytown safe?

Yes. Tarrytown is one of the safest neighborhoods in Austin, with crime rates well below the city average. The residential character, low through-traffic, and engaged community contribute to a strong sense of safety. Luke Allen notes that children routinely walk and bike freely through the neighborhood — something increasingly rare in a city of Austin's size.

What zip code is Tarrytown in?

Tarrytown is in the 78703 ZIP code — one of Austin's most sought-after ZIPs. The 78703 area also includes Clarksville, Old West Austin, and Bryker Woods. Tarrytown occupies the western portion, bounded roughly by MoPac (west), 35th Street (north), Exposition Blvd (east), and Lake Austin Blvd (south). Luke Allen specializes in 78703 and can help you understand the micro-neighborhoods within it.
