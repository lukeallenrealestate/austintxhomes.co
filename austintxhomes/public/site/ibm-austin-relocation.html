Know Before You Search

IBM's Austin Campus:
Burnet Road & US-183

IBM's primary Austin campus is at 11400 Burnet Road, Austin, TX 78758 — right at the US-183 interchange in North Austin. The Domain and Arboretum area are literally minutes away. MoPac (Loop 1) is a few blocks west. The location puts IBM employees at the hub of Austin's tech corridor, with access heading northwest toward Cedar Park and Leander, and south down Burnet Rd toward some of the city's most established neighborhoods.

That Burnet Rd south access is IBM's unique advantage. Apple employees on Riata Vista have a longer drive to get to Allandale or Crestview. IBM employees can be in those same neighborhoods in 12–18 minutes on Burnet Rd — no highway needed. This opens up a category of housing that's simply not practical for most Austin tech workers.

The IBM Advantage

IBM is one of the only Austin tech campuses with practical access to both the northwest suburbs (Cedar Park, Leander) and central Austin neighborhoods (Allandale, Crestview, Hyde Park). Most Austin tech employees have to pick one. IBM employees don't.

The Financial Upside

What Texas Taxes Mean
for Your IBM Salary

💰

No State Income Tax

Texas has zero state income tax. On a $150K IBM salary, that's $8,000–$13,500 more take-home per year vs. California or New York. IBM's Austin office draws extensively from both coasts — for employees relocating from high-tax states, the difference shows up immediately in your first paycheck and compounds with every raise and bonus.

🏠

Property Tax Reality

Texas property taxes run 1.8%–2.4% of assessed value. On a $650K home in Allandale, budget $11,700–$15,600/year ($975–$1,300/mo). Travis County (Austin proper) rates tend to be slightly higher than Williamson County (Cedar Park, Round Rock). Factor this into your mortgage affordability calculation when comparing Austin vs. suburbs.

🎯

Homestead Exemption

Texas offers a homestead exemption that reduces your home's taxable value. File with Travis CAD or Williamson CAD after January 1 of your first full year of ownership. On a $700K home, this typically saves $1,200–$1,800/year. A simple one-time filing that pays dividends annually — IBM's HR onboarding rarely mentions it.

Before You Arrive

Austin Relocation
Checklist for IBM Employees

  • Get Texas driver's license within 90 days of establishing residency
  • Register vehicle in Texas (no annual inspection required post-2025)
  • Register to vote in Travis County (Austin/Allandale) or Williamson County (Cedar Park/Leander)
  • Open a local bank account — Frost Bank, UFCU, or Amplify CU are Austin staples
  • Set up Austin Energy (city utility for Austin proper) or Pedernales Electric (Cedar Park/Leander)
  • File homestead exemption with the appropriate CAD after Jan 1 of first full year of ownership
  • Get pre-approved for a mortgage before your IBM start date if buying
  • Drive the Burnet Rd corridor south from IBM — visit Allandale and Crestview before assuming you want suburbs
  • Download TxTag app if commuting via 183A toll road to Cedar Park or Leander
  • Budget for summer electric bills — Austin AC season runs May–October, $180–$280/mo
  • Plan for Texas weather: hail insurance riders are common; research flood risk for any specific property

Common Questions

IBM Austin
Relocation FAQ

Where is IBM's Austin campus located?

IBM's primary Austin campus is at 11400 Burnet Road, Austin, TX 78758 — in North Austin at the intersection of Burnet Rd and US-183. This is just west of the Domain and Arboretum area, with direct access to MoPac (Loop 1) and US-183. The Burnet Rd corridor runs south into Allandale, Crestview, and Hyde Park — established central Austin neighborhoods within 12–18 minutes of campus.

Where do most IBM Austin employees live?

The most common areas are Cedar Park, Great Hills/Balcones, the Domain area, and Allandale/Crestview. Cedar Park and Leander via 183A are popular for families. Great Hills is the closest established neighborhood with excellent schools. Allandale and Crestview on Burnet Rd are a unique IBM advantage — walkable, characterful Austin neighborhoods within 12–18 minutes via Burnet Rd.

How long is the commute from Austin neighborhoods to IBM's campus?

Domain/Arboretum: 5–10 min. Great Hills: 8–15 min. Allandale/Crestview: 12–18 min via Burnet Rd. Cedar Park: 15–22 min via 183A. Leander: 20–30 min via 183A. Hyde Park/North Loop: 15–22 min via Burnet Rd or Lamar. Central Austin/Mueller: 18–28 min via MoPac or 183. Avoid South Austin, Kyle, Buda, and East Austin — 35–55 min in the wrong direction.

Does IBM offer relocation assistance for Austin?

IBM offers relocation packages for new hires and transfers, varying significantly by role level and business unit. Packages typically cover moving expenses; senior roles may include temporary housing allowances. Confirm specifics with IBM HR — if your package includes short-term housing, use that window to explore neighborhoods before committing to a purchase.

What makes IBM's Austin location different from other tech campuses?

IBM's Burnet Rd campus is uniquely positioned between the Domain-area tech corridor and Central Austin's established neighborhoods. Unlike Apple (further north on Riata Vista) or Dell (in Round Rock), IBM employees can realistically live in Allandale, Crestview, or Hyde Park with a sub-20 minute commute. This urban Austin option is not available to most Austin tech employees.

Are there good schools near IBM's Austin campus?

Yes. Great Hills and Allandale are served by Austin ISD — research campus-level ratings carefully as they vary within the district. Cedar Park and Leander are served by Leander ISD, one of the top-rated large districts in Texas. Hyde Park feeds into Lamar MS and McCallum HS — strong AISD campuses. Many IBM families choose Cedar Park or Leander for school quality while accepting the slightly longer commute.
