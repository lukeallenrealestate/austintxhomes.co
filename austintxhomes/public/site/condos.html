Condo Living
in Austin

Austin's condo market is one of the most diverse in Texas — from sleek downtown high-rises to boutique mid-rises in walkable neighborhoods. For buyers who want low maintenance, prime location, and urban lifestyle, a condo often beats a house.

The Process

How to Buy a Condo
in Austin

Buying a condo has a few extra steps compared to a house — here's what to expect.

01

Get Pre-Approved

Condo financing can be trickier than a single-family home. Some buildings are on restricted lists for conventional loans, and FHA/VA approval varies by building. I'll connect you with lenders who know Austin's condo market before you start touring.

02

Review the HOA

Before making an offer, we review the HOA financials, meeting minutes, reserve fund balance, and any pending special assessments. A building with poor reserves is a liability, not an asset.

03

Make a Strategic Offer

Austin's condo market is active. I'll advise on offer price based on recent comps in the specific building — not just the neighborhood — and negotiate closing costs, HOA transfer fees, and any repairs.

04

Inspect & Close

Condo inspections differ from house inspections — we focus on the unit itself, HVAC, plumbing, and any building-level issues disclosed in the HOA docs. From contract to keys, I keep everything on track.

Common Questions

Austin Condos FAQ

How much do condos cost in Austin TX?
Austin condo prices range from around $200K for smaller units in outer neighborhoods to $2M+ for high-floor units in luxury downtown buildings. The most active price range is $300K–$600K, which covers most mid-rise buildings in walkable neighborhoods.
What are typical HOA fees for Austin condos?
HOA fees vary significantly. Smaller boutique buildings run $150–$400/month. Full-service high-rises with amenities (concierge, pool, gym, parking garage) can run $600–$1,500+/month. Always factor the HOA into your total monthly payment when comparing condos to houses.
Are Austin condos a good investment?
It depends on the building. Some Austin condo buildings have appreciated strongly; others have lagged behind single-family homes. Downtown high-rises and condos in Mueller, East Austin, and South Congress have historically held value well. I'll give you honest data on specific buildings before you buy.
Can I get an FHA loan for an Austin condo?
Only condos in FHA-approved buildings qualify. Many Austin condo buildings are not FHA approved, which limits your buyer pool when you go to sell. I always check FHA and VA approval status before you fall in love with a unit.
What neighborhoods have the most condos in Austin?
Downtown Austin has the highest concentration, followed by East Austin, South Congress, Mueller, Clarksville, and The Domain. Hyde Park and Travis Heights have boutique condo conversions. The Domain is popular for buyers wanting north Austin walkability.
Do I need a Realtor to buy a condo in Austin?
You don't need one, but it costs you nothing to have one — the seller pays buyer agent commission. With condos specifically, having an agent who knows the buildings, HOA financials, and financing restrictions is especially valuable.
