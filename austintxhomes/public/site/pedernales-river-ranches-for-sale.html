Hill Country ranching

The Pedernales is ranch country.

The Pedernales corridor is one of the more concentrated Hill Country ranch markets in Central Texas. Properties run from 25-acre weekend tracts up through 500+ acre legacy operations, with the bulk of inventory in the 50 to 250 acre range. Many properties carry working cattle infrastructure, hunting improvements, or recreational features around river frontage.

Property tax matters here. Most large-acreage Pedernales properties qualify for a Texas ag or wildlife exemption that drops the assessed value to a fraction of market value, often by 80 to 95 percent. For a $5M ranch, that can translate to property tax of $3K to $8K per year instead of $80K to $100K. Maintaining the exemption requires meeting county-specific use criteria.

Ranch buyers along the Pedernales tend to be Austin tech executives buying recreational ranches, established cattle operators expanding their footprint, hunting ranches catering to Texas and out-of-state hunters, and Houston or Dallas buyers wanting a Hill Country weekend escape with serious acreage.

Diligence on a Pedernales ranch is materially heavier than diligence on a residential property. Surveys, fence-line verification, mineral rights, water rights, well capacity testing, septic compliance, brush management history, easement review, ag exemption paperwork, and floodplain analysis all matter. Working with a specialist who knows the corridor and the diligence framework is the difference between a clean close and a discovery during option that kills the deal.

Most large-acreage Pedernales properties qualify for either an agricultural or wildlife valuation. Texas requires the property to demonstrate qualifying use (livestock, hay production, wildlife management, or similar). Maintaining the exemption requires meeting county-specific criteria. Properly maintained exemptions typically drop assessed value by 80 to 95 percent of market value, dramatically reducing property tax.
