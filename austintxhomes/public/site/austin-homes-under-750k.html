Where to Look

Austin Neighborhoods
in the $600K–$750K Range

These are the City of Austin neighborhoods where $600K–$750K produces real, desirable inventory in 2026.

Travis Heights

SFH $650K–$900K · some $620K–$720K

Hillside neighborhood just south of Lady Bird Lake with Hill Country views. Bungalows, craftsman homes, and mid-century SFH on tree-lined streets. Walking distance to South Congress. One of Austin's most character-rich neighborhoods.

Hyde Park

SFH $650K–$950K · condos $500K–$700K

Austin's original historic residential neighborhood. Bungalows, Craftsman homes, old-growth trees, walkable to UT, Guadalupe Street retail, and Central Market. SFH edges into $600K–$750K for smaller or less-renovated homes.

East Austin (Best Blocks)

SFH $620K–$850K

Not the transitional blocks — the established ones. Chestnut, Cherrywood, and Govalle streets where the renovation wave already finished. Bungalows with yards and garages, walk or bike to East 6th and Rainey Street.

Bouldin Creek (edges)

SFH $700K–$1.2M · smaller homes $650K–$750K

78704's most sought-after neighborhood. Full SFH is largely above $750K, but smaller homes, older ranches, and occasional fixer-uppers surface in the $650K–$750K range. Barton Springs a 10-min walk. Strongest long-term appreciation in Austin.

Mueller

SFH $650K–$850K · townhomes $500K–$700K

Austin's best planned urban neighborhood. SFH in the $600K–$750K range here are typically 3BR, modern construction (post-2005), HOA-maintained common areas. Aldrich Street farmers market, parks, and urban walkability without the older-home maintenance surprises.

Allandale / Rosedale

SFH $650K–$950K · some $620K–$750K

North-central Austin's established mid-century ranch neighborhood. Large lots, mature trees, 1950s–60s construction. Excellent schools (Austin ISD's stronger campuses). Domain corridor is 15 min north — popular with Apple and tech employees who want central Austin living.

$750K is a strong mid-to-upper budget in Austin. With 20% down ($150K), P&I at 7% runs ~$3,990/mo. Add property taxes (~$1,100–$1,500/mo) and insurance (~$200/mo) and total monthly outlay is roughly $5,290–$5,690/mo. Household income of $200K–$230K is typical to comfortably qualify. This segment is competitive but not as frenzied as 2021–2022 — well-priced homes in great locations still attract offers quickly, but you're not facing 10-offer situations as routinely.
The jump from $500K to $750K is a neighborhood-quality shift. At $500K you're on the edges of desirable neighborhoods — transitional East Austin, outer South Austin, North Loop up-and-comers. At $750K you're on the best streets in those neighborhoods: Bouldin Creek proper, Travis Heights with views, established Hyde Park, East Austin blocks that fully gentrified. 78704 covers Bouldin Creek, Travis Heights, South Congress, South Lamar, and Barton Hills — Austin's most coveted zip code. At $750K, you can find SFH in 78704 though options are more limited than at $900K+. Expect smaller homes (1,200–1,600 sqft) or homes needing some updating. Bouldin Creek SFH trends $750K–$1.2M. The upside: 78704 has the strongest appreciation trajectory in Austin's city limits. Buying at $750K here has historically been one of the best long-term holds in the city.
An ADU (Accessory Dwelling Unit) is a secondary living unit on the same lot — a garage apartment, backyard cottage, or converted space. Austin has relatively permissive ADU ordinances, and many central Austin lots in the $600K–$750K tier have space for one. A well-built ADU can rent for $1,500–$2,500/mo, effectively offsetting a significant portion of your mortgage.
