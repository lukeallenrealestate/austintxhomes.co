Know Before You Search

Dell's Campus Is in
Round Rock, Not Austin

Dell Technologies' global headquarters sits at One Dell Way, Round Rock, TX 78682. Round Rock is its own city — home to 145,000 people, excellent schools, and a thriving local economy — immediately north of Austin on I-35. The city has grown substantially around Dell's presence over the past 30 years and has some of the best infrastructure for a Dell commute of anywhere in the metro.

The practical upside: living in Round Rock means you may never need to get on a freeway. Many Dell employees live within 10 minutes via local roads. Neighborhoods that sound "Austin adjacent" on paper — Cedar Park, Georgetown, Pflugerville — are all closer to Dell than downtown Austin. Meanwhile, South Austin, Lakeway, and the Domain corridor are in completely the wrong direction for the Dell commute.

The Financial Upside

What Texas Taxes Mean
for Your Dell Salary

💰

No State Income Tax

Texas has zero state income tax. On a $130K Dell salary, that's $7,000–$10,500 more take-home per year vs. California. For Dell employees relocating from the Bay Area or Seattle, this shows up immediately and meaningfully — often covering the property tax difference entirely within the first two years.

🏠

Property Tax Reality

Texas property taxes run 1.8%–2.4% of assessed value. On a $460K home in Round Rock, budget $8,280–$11,040/year ($690–$920/mo). Round Rock and Williamson County rates are mid-range for Texas. Factor this into your mortgage calculation — lenders will include it in your DTI from day one.

🎯

Homestead Exemption

Texas offers a homestead exemption that reduces your home's taxable assessed value. File after January 1 of your first full year of ownership. In Williamson County (Round Rock, Georgetown, Cedar Park), this typically saves $900–$1,500/year — money back annually with zero ongoing effort after the initial filing.

  • Get Texas driver's license within 90 days of establishing residency

Where do most Dell employees live near Round Rock?

Round Rock itself is the most popular — many Dell employees live within 5–15 minutes of campus. Georgetown is a close second, especially for employees who want more space or a quieter setting. Cedar Park and Pflugerville are both within 20–25 minutes. North Austin is viable for employees who want city amenities with a reasonable I-35 commute.

How long is the commute from Austin to Dell headquarters?

From Round Rock itself: 5–15 min. Georgetown: 15–22 min via I-35. Cedar Park: 18–25 min via 183A/I-35. Pflugerville: 15–22 min via SH-130. North Austin: 20–30 min via I-35. Central Austin: 28–42 min depending on traffic. Avoid South Austin, Kyle, Buda, and Lakeway — those run 45–65 min each way.

Does Dell offer relocation assistance?

Dell Technologies offers relocation packages for new hires and internal transfers, particularly for professional, engineering, and management roles. Package details vary by level. Relocation typically covers moving expenses and may include a lump-sum allowance. Confirm your specific package with Dell HR — if it includes temporary housing, use that window to evaluate neighborhoods before buying.

What is the cost of living near Dell in Round Rock?

Round Rock is one of Austin's best-value suburbs. Typical buy prices range from $360K–$580K for single-family homes. Georgetown runs $340K–$560K. Cedar Park $380K–$580K. Pflugerville $320K–$500K. Combined with Texas's zero state income tax, a Dell salary goes significantly further here than in comparable tech markets like Silicon Valley or Seattle.

Are the schools good near Dell's campus in Round Rock?

Round Rock ISD is one of the top-rated large school districts in Central Texas — and you're already in it if you live in Round Rock. Georgetown ISD is growing and improving. Leander ISD (Cedar Park) is consistently rated one of the best large districts in Texas. All three are strong options for Dell families relocating with children.
