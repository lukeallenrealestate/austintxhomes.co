What Makes This Market Different

Horseshoe Bay real estate runs on different rules than Austin.

The buyer pool is mostly out-of-market.

Roughly 60–70% of Horseshoe Bay transactions are second-home or weekend-home buyers from Austin, Houston, Dallas, and San Antonio. That changes everything: marketing, showing logistics, financing structures, even the days-on-market a listing should expect. A primary-residence playbook from Austin doesn't translate.

Country club access is part of the asset.

The Horseshoe Bay Resort & Country Club operates a tiered membership structure. Many properties convey with membership rights, but not all do — and the category (Premier vs. Cap Rock) materially affects what amenities you get. Luke verifies membership status, transfer fees, and category in every transaction before close.

Dock categories aren't equal.

"Waterfront" means very different things in Horseshoe Bay. Open Lake LBJ frontage on the main body commands the highest premiums. Protected coves have calmer water but limit boat-size flexibility. Some lots have shared community docks rather than private. The category determines value — sometimes by hundreds of thousands.

Lake LBJ is constant-level — that's the asset.

Unlike Lake Travis, which fluctuates 50+ feet seasonally and can leave docks high-and-dry during droughts, Lake LBJ stays at a stable elevation year-round (regulated by upstream dams). That single fact is why serious boat owners prefer Lake LBJ: your dock works in August AND March. It's also why Lake LBJ waterfront has been more recession-resistant.

Schools depend on which side you're on.

Horseshoe Bay straddles two school districts: Marble Falls ISD on the south side, Llano ISD on the north. Most full-time families are on the Marble Falls ISD side. If schools matter to your purchase, the parcel determines the district — confirm before offer.

Tax is meaningfully lower than Austin.

Burnet and Llano county property tax rates run 1.6–1.9% vs. Austin's 2.0–2.4%. On a $2M home, that's $8K–$12K saved per year. Combined with Texas's zero state income tax, it's one of the most tax-efficient luxury markets in the country.

Horseshoe Bay Micro-Markets

Where to look in Horseshoe Bay.

Horseshoe Bay is a small city by population but covers a meaningful geography of distinctly different sub-markets. Each area has its own price profile, lifestyle, and resale dynamics. Luke maps buyers to the right sub-area based on their use case, budget, and tolerance for HOA/membership structures.

Apple Rock Golf Course

$700K – $3M

The original RTJ Sr. course at Horseshoe Bay. Course-frontage homes carry steady premium and the most consistent resale demand. Mature trees, established streets, family-oriented atmosphere.

Slick Rock Golf Course

$650K – $2.5M

The second RTJ Sr. course. More elevation change, dramatic terrain, and panoramic Hill Country views from elevated lots. Slightly less crowded than Apple Rock.

Ram Rock Golf Course

$600K – $2.2M

The third RTJ Sr. course — the most challenging of the three and a strong choice for serious players. Course-side inventory is smallest, which protects values when supply tightens.

Lake LBJ Open Waterfront

$1.5M – $10M+

Premium open-water frontage on the main body of Lake LBJ — the highest-end inventory in the city. Private dock, deep water, panoramic views, and the strongest long-term value retention. Limited supply.

Lake LBJ Protected Coves

$900K – $2.8M

Cove waterfront — calmer water, easier dock construction, family-friendly swimming. Better entry point for waterfront buyers vs. open lake. Some coves are quieter (better for full-time living), others are busier (better rental potential).

Cape Esperanza & Summit Rock

$500K – $1.5M

Newer luxury subdivisions with custom Hill Country homes, golf and lake access without dock ownership. Strong fit for lock-and-leave second-home buyers who want resort amenities but not waterfront maintenance.

Resort Condominiums

$400K – $1.1M

Lock-and-leave condos with on-resort amenities — strongest short-term-rental income potential and lowest carrying costs. Great option for first-time second-home buyers testing the market.

The Peninsula / Trail's End

$700K – $2M

Quieter pockets with mature landscaping and full-time-resident character. Less foot-traffic than the resort core but full club access for members. A good fit for buyers who want Horseshoe Bay community without resort-area noise.
