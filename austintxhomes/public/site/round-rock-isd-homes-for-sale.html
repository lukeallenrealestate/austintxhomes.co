The School District

Why Families Choose
Round Rock ISD

Round Rock ISD is one of the best-kept secrets in the Austin area — except it's not really a secret anymore. With a TEA A rating, over 50,000 students, and Westwood High School consistently ranked among the top 5 public high schools in Texas, RRISD delivers elite school quality at a fraction of what you'd pay in other top-rated Austin-area districts.

The district serves Round Rock, parts of north Austin, portions of Cedar Park, and a small section of Pflugerville. It's geographically large and diverse — from established neighborhoods in central Round Rock with mature trees and walkable streets to brand-new master-planned communities in the north with resort-style amenities and modern construction.

What makes RRISD stand out among large Texas districts is the combination of academic rigor and accessibility. Strong STEM programs run through every level. The district's diversity — economic, ethnic, and cultural — prepares students for the real world in ways that smaller, more homogeneous districts simply cannot. And the proximity to major tech employers like Dell Technologies and Samsung means the parent community is highly educated and engaged.

For buyers, Round Rock ISD represents the best value proposition in the Austin metro. You can get into the district for $300K–$400K and access the same caliber of education that costs $900K+ in Eanes ISD. Homes in the Westwood HS feeder zone — Walsh Ranch, Behrens Ranch — command a premium within the district but still undercut comparable school quality elsewhere.

If you want top schools without top-dollar pricing, this is your district. Browse Round Rock neighborhoods, explore new construction options, or reach out to talk through the market.

Market Reality

What Buying in Round Rock ISD
Actually Looks Like

Round Rock ISD is one of the most active real estate markets in the Austin metro — and one of the most varied. Unlike Eanes ISD, which is geographically tiny and uniformly expensive, RRISD spans dozens of neighborhoods across multiple price tiers. That means more options, but also more complexity in figuring out where you'll get the most value.

At the $300K–$450K level, you're looking at central Round Rock, older sections of Cat Hollow, and parts of Pflugerville that feed into the district. These homes move quickly — first-time buyers and investors compete in this range. Expect multiple offers on well-priced properties and limited room for concessions.

The $450K–$700K sweet spot is where most families land. Walsh Ranch, Behrens Ranch, and Brushy Creek offer the combination of newer construction, excellent feeder zones (particularly Westwood HS), and the community amenities that relocation buyers expect. Homes in this range typically sell within 2–3 weeks if priced correctly.

Above $700K, you're in the premium tier — Paloma Lake, Teravista's larger lots, and custom builds in newer sections. Days on market extend to 30–60 days, and buyers have more negotiating leverage. This tier often attracts Dell and Samsung executives who want space and school quality without the Westlake price tag.

The key insight: Round Rock ISD's new construction pipeline in north Round Rock keeps the market more balanced than supply-constrained districts like Eanes. But the established neighborhoods — particularly in the Westwood HS zone — behave more like a seller's market because no one is building new homes there.

Common Questions

Round Rock ISD FAQ

What cities and neighborhoods are in Round Rock ISD?
Round Rock ISD serves the city of Round Rock, parts of north Austin, portions of Cedar Park, and a small section of Pflugerville. Primary zip codes include 78664, 78665, 78681, 78717, 78729, and 78750. Popular neighborhoods include Forest Creek, Brushy Creek, Walsh Ranch, Behrens Ranch, Sendero Springs, Paloma Lake, Teravista, and Cat Hollow.
What are the best schools in Round Rock ISD?
Round Rock ISD has six high schools. Westwood HS is consistently ranked among the top 5 public high schools in Texas. Other strong campuses include McNeil HS, Round Rock HS, Stony Point HS, Cedar Ridge HS, and Dragon Ranch HS. Notable middle schools include Walsh, Canyon Vista, Deerpark, and Cedar Valley. Top elementary schools include Spicewood, Forest Creek, Brushy Creek, and Great Oaks.
What is the typical home price range in Round Rock ISD?
Homes in Round Rock ISD typically range from $300K to $1M. Central Round Rock starts around $300K–$500K. Forest Creek and Cat Hollow run $350K–$550K. Premium communities like Walsh Ranch and Behrens Ranch range from $450K–$700K. Newer developments like Paloma Lake and Teravista top out at $500K–$900K. The district offers strong value relative to school quality.
How does Round Rock ISD compare to other Austin-area districts?
Round Rock ISD is TEA A-rated and one of the highest-rated large districts in Texas with over 50,000 students. Westwood HS rivals Eanes ISD's Westlake High in academic rankings. The key difference is price — you can access comparable school quality for $300K–$700K in RRISD vs. $900K–$4M in Eanes. The district is also more diverse and offers strong STEM programs across all grade levels.
Is Round Rock ISD a good area for tech workers?
Excellent. Dell Technologies' global headquarters is in Round Rock, and Samsung's semiconductor fab is in north Austin — both within 5–15 minutes of most RRISD neighborhoods. Many tech workers choose Round Rock specifically for the short commute, top-rated schools, and more affordable housing. The 20–35 minute commute to downtown Austin via I-35 or MoPac is also manageable.
Can I commute to downtown Austin from Round Rock ISD?
Yes. Most Round Rock ISD neighborhoods are 20–35 minutes from downtown Austin via I-35 or MoPac outside of peak hours. Rush-hour commutes can extend to 40–50 minutes. Many residents commute to the Domain area (15–20 minutes) or to Dell and Samsung campuses nearby (5–15 minutes). Capital Metro express bus routes serve some Round Rock corridors as well.
