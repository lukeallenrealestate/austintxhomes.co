Two Paths

Rule #3 vs Rule #4 — Which Is Right for You?

Texas law provides two property-based paths to residency. Both work — the right choice depends on your family's situation.

Most Common

Rule #3: Student Lives in Condo

  • Purchase condo deeded in student's name
  • Student lives there for 12+ months
  • Utility bills in student's name
  • Texas DL + voter registration
  • File federal taxes independently
  • Submit: deed, utilities, tax return, TX DL, voter card

Best for: Families who want their student to have their own place near campus. The condo builds equity while saving on tuition.

Rule #4: Student Owns Rental LLC

  • Purchase rental property for student to manage
  • Student creates LLC ($300, online via TX Secretary of State)
  • LLC has business checking account
  • Texas DL + voter registration
  • File federal taxes independently
  • Submit: deed, LLC cert, 12mo bank stmts, tax return, TX DL, voter card, TX lease proof

Best for: Families who want a pure investment play — the student can live in a dorm or apartment while managing the rental property as a business.
