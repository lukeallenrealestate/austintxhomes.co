The School District

Why Families Choose
Lake Travis ISD

Lake Travis ISD is one of the fastest-growing and highest-rated school districts in Central Texas. TEA A-rated and consistently ranked in the top 5 across the Austin metro, LTISD draws families who want excellent schools without the price premium of neighboring Eanes ISD - and with significantly more home for their money.

The district serves Lakeway, Bee Cave, Spicewood, and unincorporated portions of Travis and Hays County west of Austin. It covers a large geographic area along the south shore of Lake Travis, stretching from the 620 corridor through the Hill Country and out toward Marble Falls.

Lake Travis High School is the district's flagship - a 6A campus with nationally ranked athletics (the football program is legendary, with multiple state championships) and strong academics including AP, dual-enrollment, and career and technical programs. The middle and elementary schools are similarly well-regarded, with parent involvement that rivals any district in the state.

For buyers, Lake Travis ISD offers a wide price range that Eanes simply doesn't. You can find newer construction in the $400K range in Bee Cave and Serene Hills, or you can spend $3M+ on a lakefront estate in Spanish Oaks. That range means buyers at every price point have options - something that's increasingly rare in top-rated Austin-area districts.

If you're looking at Hill Country living with lake access and top-rated schools, this is the district. Browse listings in Lakeway, explore luxury homes in Austin, or reach out to talk through the market.

Neighborhoods Within the District

  • Lakeway
    The heart of the district. Golf courses, tennis, community pools, live oaks, and a mix of 1980s–2000s homes alongside new builds. Most homes are $600K–$1.2M.
  • Bee Cave / Serene Hills
    Newer construction, family-friendly neighborhoods with quick access to Bee Cave Rd and Hill Country Galleria shopping. Entry point for LTISD buyers. $400K–$700K.
  • Rough Hollow
    Resort-style master-planned community with a private marina, pools, fitness center, and waterfront dining. Custom builds and semi-custom homes. $700K–$1.5M.
  • Flintrock Falls
    Golf course community within Lakeway. Custom homes with Hill Country views, mature landscaping, and the Flintrock Falls Country Club. $700K–$1.5M.
  • Spanish Oaks / Lakefront
    Gated luxury. Spanish Oaks features a private golf course and custom estates on large lots. Lakefront properties command the district's highest prices. $1.5M–$5M+.

Market Reality

What Buying in Lake Travis ISD
Actually Looks Like

The Lake Travis ISD market is diverse - and that's its biggest advantage. Unlike neighboring Eanes ISD, where entry-level homes start near $900K, Lake Travis ISD has genuine options at the $400K–$500K level in newer Bee Cave and Serene Hills subdivisions. That breadth means first-time buyers, move-up families, and luxury buyers are all active in this market simultaneously.

At the $400K–$700K range, expect newer construction with modern floor plans - many built in the last 5–10 years. These neighborhoods are popular with young families who want LTISD schools without stretching to seven figures. Competition is steady but not frenzied. Homes in this range typically sell within 30–45 days if priced correctly.

The $700K–$1.5M sweet spot is where Lakeway core, Rough Hollow, and Flintrock Falls live. These are established communities with resort-style amenities, larger lots, and mature landscaping. Buyers here are often moving up from starter homes or relocating from out of state with strong purchasing power. Days on market range from 20–45 depending on condition and pricing precision.

Above $1.5M, you enter the luxury tier - Spanish Oaks estates, lakefront properties, and custom builds on multi-acre lots. This segment moves more slowly, with 60–120 days on market being common. But the properties are exceptional: private golf, Lake Travis waterfront, Hill Country panoramas. Having an agent who understands luxury pricing in this specific submarket is critical.

The lakefront premium in Lake Travis ISD is significant - waterfront access can add 50–100% to a comparable inland property's price. If lake access is important but direct waterfront is out of budget, communities like Rough Hollow offer marina access and waterfront amenities at a fraction of the lakefront price. I can help you navigate these tradeoffs and find the right fit.

Common Questions

Lake Travis ISD FAQ

What cities and neighborhoods are in Lake Travis ISD?
Lake Travis ISD serves Lakeway, Bee Cave, Spicewood, and portions of unincorporated Travis and Hays County west of Austin. Primary zip codes are 78738, 78734, 78669, 78732, and 78733. Neighborhoods include Rough Hollow, Flintrock Falls, The Hills, Sweetwater, Serene Hills, Spanish Oaks, Falconhead, and Cardinal Hills. The district is one of the fastest-growing in Central Texas.
What are the schools in Lake Travis ISD?
Lake Travis ISD has multiple elementary schools (Lake Travis ES, Lakeway ES, Bee Cave ES, Lake Pointe ES, Serene Hills ES), three middle schools (Lake Travis MS, Hudson Bend MS, Bee Cave MS), and Lake Travis High School - a 6A campus nationally recognized for its football program, strong academics, and competitive extracurriculars. Hudson Bend HS serves as an alternative campus.
What is the typical home price range in Lake Travis ISD?
Homes in Lake Travis ISD range from approximately $400K to $5M+. Newer construction in Bee Cave and Serene Hills starts around $400K–$700K. Established Lakeway neighborhoods run $600K–$1.2M. Resort-style communities like Rough Hollow and Flintrock Falls are $700K–$1.5M. Gated luxury communities like Spanish Oaks and lakefront properties top out at $1.5M–$5M+.
How does Lake Travis ISD compare to Eanes ISD?
Both are TEA A-rated and rank in the top 5 in Central Texas. Lake Travis ISD offers comparable academic quality at a lower price point - you'll get more square footage and newer construction for your budget. Lake Travis also has lake access and resort-style community amenities that Eanes doesn't. Eanes edges ahead in some rankings, but Lake Travis is closing the gap and growing rapidly.
Is Lake Travis ISD a good investment?
Lake Travis ISD is one of the fastest-growing districts in Central Texas, which has driven consistent home value appreciation. New commercial development along 71 and in Bee Cave is expanding the local economy. Top-rated schools, lake access, Hill Country setting, and proximity to Austin make LTISD homes attractive to families and investors. Newer subdivisions in the $400K–$700K range offer especially strong long-term upside.
Can I commute to downtown Austin from Lake Travis ISD?
Yes, though it's a longer commute than inner-city neighborhoods. Most Lake Travis ISD areas are 25–40 minutes from downtown Austin via Bee Cave Rd/Highway 71 or RR 620. Morning rush hour can push that to 45–55 minutes. Many residents commute to the Domain tech corridor or work remotely. The tradeoff is Hill Country views, lake access, and significantly more home for your money compared to central Austin.
