🌡️January avg high: 61°F  ·  Mild, rarely below 35°F
☀️~300 sunny days per year  ·  July avg high: 97°F

Both cities have hot summers — but DC's heat is compounded by swamp-level humidity that makes outdoor life genuinely unpleasant June through September. Austin's 97°F July is dry heat by comparison: 40–50% humidity versus DC's 80–90%. The winters tell a clearer story: DC's gray, damp, occasionally icy winters vs. Austin's mild and sunny January.

The commute comparison: DC's Beltway and I-66 are consistently ranked among America's worst commutes. Austin's I-35 is notorious — but the city is smaller, many neighborhoods are 10–20 minutes from downtown, and Austin's tech campuses are genuinely distributed. Most Austin residents have commutes under 25 minutes. Most Northern Virginia residents have commutes over 45 minutes as a baseline.

For Families

Austin Schools
for DC-Area Families

Northern Virginia Context

Fairfax County Public Schools, Langley HS, and Thomas Jefferson S&T are among the most competitive public schools in the country. DC-area families have high school expectations. The Austin equivalent for consistent top-tier public education is Eanes ISD — not AISD at the district level. Research is required to find AISD's strongest campuses.

Austin ISD — Central

AISD is highly variable by campus. Strong elementaries: Bryker Woods, Casis, Barton Hills, Travis Heights. High schools: McCallum (Hyde Park) and Bowie (South Austin) are consistent performers. Research by specific campus address — the district's average understates what the best campuses offer, and overstates others.

Eanes ISD — Westlake

The closest Austin equivalent to Fairfax County's best schools or Montgomery County MD. Westlake HS ranks in Texas's top 5 consistently. Located in Westlake Hills, 15–20 min from downtown. Homes $900K–$2M+. The non-negotiable option for families accustomed to NoVa's premium school districts. Worth the Westlake price premium.

Round Rock / Leander ISD

For tech employees joining Domain-area employers, Round Rock ISD and Leander ISD are solid — comparable to Fairfax County's middle tier. Cedar Park, Round Rock, and Leander offer good schools with more home per dollar than Westlake. The right trade for Domain-corridor families who want suburban quality without Westlake pricing.

Common Questions

DC to Austin
FAQ

How much do I save on taxes moving from Northern Virginia or DC to Austin?

Virginia has a 5.75% income tax on all income over $17,000. DC's income tax runs 4%–10.75% with an effective rate of 8%+ for most professionals. Texas has zero. On $200K income: Virginia residents save ~$11,500/year; DC residents save ~$16,000/year. On $400K: Virginia saves ~$23,000; DC residents save ~$34,000. The DC savings are among the largest of any relocation corridor we cover — DC's progressive rates rival New York City's.

What Austin neighborhood is most like Capitol Hill or Dupont Circle?

Capitol Hill DC maps closely to East Austin — walkable, historically rooted, evolving character, bars and restaurants throughout. Dupont Circle and Logan Circle translate to Hyde Park and Rosedale — walkable, established neighborhood identity, tree-lined streets, community institutions. Georgetown buyers typically land in Clarksville or Tarrytown. McLean and Bethesda families go to Westlake Hills.

Is Austin a good move for federal contractors or government employees?

Yes. Austin has a significant and growing defense tech, aerospace, and federal contractor presence — SpaceX Starbase operations, Applied Research Laboratories at UT Austin, Dell Federal, L3Harris, Firefly Aerospace, and numerous defense tech startups. Many federal employees and contractors moving to Austin transition into this private-sector ecosystem, or work remotely for existing agencies or contractors.

How does Northern Virginia housing compare to Austin?

Northern Virginia has been sustained by government contracting premiums for decades. Arlington and Alexandria 3BR homes run $800K–$1.4M; McLean and Great Falls reach $1.2M–$3M+. Austin's comparable neighborhoods — East Austin, Hyde Park, Barton Hills — run $550K–$1.1M. Texas property taxes are higher (1.8–2.4% vs. Virginia's ~1%), but income tax savings exceed the property tax differential at $200K+ income.

How are Austin summers compared to DC?

Both cities are hot in summer — but DC combines 90°F heat with 80–90% humidity that makes outdoor life genuinely miserable June through September. Austin reaches 97°F but at 40–50% humidity — hot but functional. DC transplants almost universally find Austin's summer more manageable than DC's, despite higher temperatures. Austin's winters (61°F January average) are dramatically better than DC's cold, gray, occasionally icy winters.

How are Austin schools for DC-area families?

Northern Virginia has some of the best public schools in the country — Langley HS, McLean HS, Thomas Jefferson S&T. The Austin equivalent for consistent top-tier public school quality is Eanes ISD (Westlake Hills): Westlake HS is in Texas's top 5. AISD's strongest campuses are in Hyde Park, South Austin, and Zilker zones. Research by campus address — AISD's district average dramatically understates what its best schools offer, and overstates others.
