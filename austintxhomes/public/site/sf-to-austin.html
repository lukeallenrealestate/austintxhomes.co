The Tax Case for Leaving California

California's 13.3%
vs. Texas Zero

California's top marginal income tax rate is 13.3% — the highest state income tax rate in the United States. It kicks in at $1M, but the effective rate at Bay Area FAANG compensation levels is punishing well below that threshold. California taxes all income: wages, RSU vests, bonuses, capital gains. Texas has no state income tax, period. The savings at senior engineer compensation levels are substantial enough that Oracle, Tesla, and Hewlett Packard Enterprise made the same calculation at the corporate level.

The Bay Area-to-Austin housing gap is the most dramatic of any migration corridor. A Palo Alto home at $3M compares to a Westlake Hills home at $1.3M — same school quality tier, same suburban character. A Noe Valley home at $2.2M compares to a central Austin home (Hyde Park, Clarksville) at $750K. The property tax rate is higher in Texas, but on a home half the price, the absolute tax bill is often lower. Most Bay Area transplants describe the first Austin home purchase as "buying the same life for a third of the cost."

A Bay Area engineer buying in Palo Alto at $3M (20% down: $600K, 30yr at 6.5%) pays ~$15,200/month PITI. The same buyer in Westlake Hills at $1.2M pays ~$8,100/month — and pockets ~$92,000/year in state income tax savings at $800K TC. The combined delta is transformative.

Austin, Texas

  • 🏊 Barton Springs Pool: cold spring-fed swimming, open year-round

Bay Area transplants have a different weather trade than Seattle or Minneapolis transplants. The Bay Area isn't brutal — it's just that SF's famous summer fog means July and August are often the coldest, grayest months of the year. Most Bay Area tech workers adapted by working indoors year-round. Austin's 97°F summer is honest heat: you plan around it with AC, Barton Springs, and morning runs. The outdoor access is different — limestone instead of redwoods — but Lady Bird Lake and the Greenbelt hit immediately.

The heat is real. Austin runs AC May through October at $180–$280/month. Budget for it. But most Bay Area transplants describe the first Austin winter — 61°F and sunny in January — as revelatory. No more Raynaud's.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked
Questions

How much do I save on taxes moving from California to Texas?

California's top marginal income tax rate is 13.3% (kicks in at $1M; 12.3% at $677K; 11.3% at $406K). Texas has no state income tax. For a $500K TC Bay Area engineer, the approximate annual CA state tax bill is ~$54,900 — and $0 in Texas. For $800K TC, the savings approach $92,400 annually. These figures depend on filing status, deductions, and whether income is salary vs. RSU vests. Consult a CPA before moving for timing guidance.

Does California try to tax me after I move to Texas?

Yes, California aggressively pursues high-income movers. CA will tax all income earned while you were a California resident (including RSU vests that occurred before your move). To establish Texas domicile, you need: Texas driver's license, voter registration, physical presence majority of the year, no California home or rent, primary bank account in Texas. High-income movers with significant unvested RSUs should work with a CPA experienced in CA domicile changes before moving.

Where do Apple Austin employees live?

Apple's Austin campus is in North Austin near the Domain — the same tech corridor as Google, Meta, Nvidia, and Amazon. Apple Austin employees typically live in Domain-adjacent neighborhoods: Avery Ranch, North Austin, Cedar Park, or Round Rock. For families who won't compromise on schools, Westlake Hills (Eanes ISD) is the Palo Alto equivalent — 25–30 minutes from the Domain. NW Hills is a middle ground: central Austin feel, reasonable Domain commute, strong AISD campuses.

How does Austin housing compare to the Bay Area?

The gap is the largest of any migration corridor. A Palo Alto 3BR runs $2.5M–$4M; the Austin equivalent (Westlake Hills, Eanes ISD) runs $900K–$1.5M. A Noe Valley 3BR in SF runs $1.8M–$2.5M; the Austin equivalent (Bouldin Creek, Hyde Park) runs $600K–$950K. Texas property tax rates are higher (1.8–2.4% vs. CA's ~1.1%), but on a home at half the price, the absolute annual tax bill is often similar or lower.

Is Austin actually comparable to San Francisco culturally?

Austin and SF have more overlap than the coasts-vs-Texas framing suggests — both are tech-heavy, liberal, outdoor-oriented, and deeply into food and music. The differences: Austin is louder, more car-dependent (no BART equivalent), Southern/Western in culture, and defined by live music rather than tech celebrity. Most SF transplants cite the outdoor access (Lady Bird Lake, Greenbelt, Barton Springs) and the community of fellow transplants as what makes it work. East Austin specifically has a strong SF expat community.

Oracle and Tesla moved their HQs to Austin — does that help me get a job there?

Oracle's Austin HQ is a full headquarters relocation — Oracle's largest concentration of employees is now in Austin. Tesla's Austin presence includes Gigafactory Texas (manufacturing and engineering) plus corporate roles. Google, Apple, Meta, and Nvidia have large offices in the Domain corridor. The Austin tech job market is thinner than the Bay Area overall, but for senior engineers willing to be flexible on company, it's viable.
