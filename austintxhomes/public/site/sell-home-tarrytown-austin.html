Insider Knowledge

What Tarrytown Buyers
Are Looking For

Tarrytown buyers are not looking for the same things as buyers in most Austin neighborhoods, and understanding that difference is critical to selling your home for top dollar. Luke Allen has worked with enough Tarrytown buyers to know exactly what they prioritize — and it is not granite countertops and stainless steel appliances. Tarrytown buyers value character over generic renovation. Original hardwood floors, mature live oaks, big porches, and the kind of architectural details that cannot be replicated in new construction — these are the things that make Tarrytown buyers pull the trigger and write aggressive offers.

Tarrytown buyers pay premiums for walkability to Casis Elementary and Deep Eddy Pool. They care about lot size — not just for the yard, but for future ADU potential under Austin's current land development code. They want natural light, established landscaping, and the feeling that a home has been part of the neighborhood for decades. Luke Allen knows how to position your Tarrytown home to highlight exactly these qualities, because they are what Tarrytown buyers will pay a premium for.

Here is something most listing agents will not tell you: over-modernized flips with gray LVP flooring and white shaker cabinets actually underperform in Tarrytown compared to homes with thoughtful, character-appropriate updates. Tarrytown buyers are savvy — they have toured the flips, they recognize the builder-grade finishes, and they would rather buy a home with original charm and invest in their own updates over time.
