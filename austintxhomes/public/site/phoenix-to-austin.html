Arizona vs. Texas Income Tax

Arizona's 2.5% Flat Rate
vs. Texas Zero

Arizona's flat income tax rate of 2.5% applies to all income — a significant simplification from the prior bracket structure, and meaningfully lower than most states. But Texas is still zero. For Phoenix and Scottsdale residents earning $200K–$600K, the annual tax savings from moving to Texas range from $5,000 to $15,000 per year. Over a 20-year career, that's $100,000 to $300,000 — real money, even if it's not the 9.9% Oregon story. The honest note: Arizona's property tax rate (~0.6–0.7%) is significantly lower than Texas (~1.8–2.4%), which partially offsets income tax savings depending on your home value.

The AZ → TX Property Tax Trade-Off

Arizona property taxes (~0.6–0.7%) are among the lowest in the US — significantly lower than Texas (~1.8–2.4%). On a $700K home, the annual property tax delta is roughly $8,400–$12,600 TX vs. $4,200–$4,900 AZ — a difference of ~$4,000–$8,000/year. At income levels below $300K, this property tax delta can offset most or all of the income tax savings. At $400K+ income, income tax savings still win clearly. Run your specific numbers with a CPA.

Arizona's Employers in Austin

TSMC, Intel & the Arizona
Semiconductor Corridor

Arizona's semiconductor cluster — TSMC Chandler, Intel Ocotillo, Microchip Technology — and Austin's cluster form connected nodes of the same industry. For engineers in the Phoenix metro, the Austin opportunity is concrete.

TSMC / Semiconductor Industry

TSMC's massive Chandler fab ($65B investment, N3/N2 process fabs) is Arizona's semiconductor anchor. TSMC has no Austin fab — but the ecosystem connects: Austin hosts Intel, Samsung (Taylor fab, 30 min north), NXP Semiconductors, Cirrus Logic, Silicon Labs, and Microchip Technology Austin offices. TSMC Arizona engineers moving to Austin often find work in this broader semiconductor ecosystem or at Austin's chip design companies.

Intel Chandler → Intel Austin

Intel's Ocotillo campus in Chandler is a major fab facility with 10,000+ Arizona employees. Intel has a meaningful Austin engineering presence — semiconductor R&D and design. This is one of the cleaner Phoenix-to-Austin employer transfer pathways in the semiconductor space. The Domain corridor in NW Austin is the natural landing zone for Intel Austin employees.

Remote Tech & Financial Services

Phoenix hosts a large contingent of remote tech workers who relocated from Bay Area and Seattle in 2020–2022. Some are now making a second move to Austin for job density. Financial services (State Farm, Fidelity, USAA Phoenix) connect to Austin — USAA has a large Austin/San Antonio footprint; Fidelity has significant Austin offices. Defense and aerospace (Raytheon, Boeing Mesa, Honeywell Phoenix) connect to Austin's L3Harris and Lockheed presence.

Austin Tech Scene

Austin's employer density — Apple (15K+ employees, north campus), Google, Meta, Nvidia, Oracle HQ, Tesla HQ, Dell HQ, Amazon — now rivals Phoenix for employment access at senior engineer levels, with significantly higher tech concentration. For semiconductor engineers specifically, Samsung's Taylor fab (30 min north of Austin, opening in phases) is a major new employer in the state.

For TSMC Chandler engineers: TSMC's Taylor, Texas fab (Williamson County, ~30 min north of Austin) is under construction and will be hiring across multiple process technology roles. This creates a direct Phoenix → Austin semiconductor pathway that didn't exist two years ago. The Domain corridor and Cedar Park are the natural landing zones — 25–35 min from the Taylor fab site.

Most Phoenix transplants miss the desert after a few years; most don't miss the commute.

Before You Arrive

Austin Relocation
Checklist for Phoenix & Scottsdale Transplants

  • Establish Texas residency — AZ taxes income of AZ residents; Texas domicile change ends Arizona's income tax claim on your earnings going forward
  • Get Texas driver's license within 90 days of establishing TX residency
  • Register vehicle in Texas (no emissions test; no state inspection post-2025)
  • File Arizona part-year resident return for your move year
  • Model your property tax delta carefully — TX rates (~1.8–2.4%) vs. AZ (~0.6–0.7%) add meaningful annual cost that partially offsets income tax savings at some income/price combinations
  • File homestead exemption after January 1 of first full year of ownership — saves $500–$1,000+/yr on your Austin property tax bill
  • Budget for humidity adjustment — Austin's August is different from Phoenix's August; both are hot but Phoenix residents often find Austin's humidity more physically demanding initially
  • Austin does get storms — not Phoenix's monsoon season style, but spring hail and occasional flash flooding; get proper hail coverage on your home and vehicles
  • Get pre-approved before your Austin search — East Austin and Hyde Park move faster than comparable Phoenix neighborhoods
  • Barton Springs Park Pass ($30/yr): your year-round outdoor swimming membership — the first thing to get after your TX license

Frequently Asked Questions

Phoenix to Austin
Common Questions

Is Arizona's income tax savings significant enough to justify moving to Texas?

Arizona's 2.5% flat rate saves real money — $6,250/yr at $250K income, $10,000/yr at $400K. But Arizona's property tax rate (~0.6–0.7%) is significantly lower than Texas (~1.8–2.4%). On a $700K home, the property tax delta runs ~$8,400–$12,000/yr in Texas vs. ~$4,200–$4,900 in Arizona. At $400K+ income, Austin's income tax savings win clearly. Most Phoenix transplants move for job opportunity and lifestyle reasons; the tax savings are a bonus.

What's the connection between Arizona's semiconductor industry and Austin?

Arizona's semiconductor cluster (TSMC Chandler, Intel Ocotillo, Microchip Technology) and Austin's cluster (Samsung Taylor, Intel Austin, NXP, Cirrus Logic, Silicon Labs) are part of the same industry ecosystem. TSMC's Taylor, TX fab — under construction 30 min north of Austin — creates a direct TSMC Arizona → Texas pathway. Intel Chandler engineers routinely transfer to Intel's Austin engineering presence. The Domain corridor is the natural tech landing zone.

How does Austin's outdoor scene compare to Phoenix's?

Phoenix has exceptional desert hiking (Camelback, South Mountain, McDowell Sonoran Preserve) and unmatched golf (300+ courses in Scottsdale metro). Austin has water: Barton Springs Pool (68°F, open year-round), Lady Bird Lake, Barton Creek Greenbelt, and Hill Country day trips. Both cities get ~300 sunny days. You're not trading good outdoor access for bad — you're trading desert and golf for water and music. Most Phoenix transplants miss Sedona; most adapt quickly to Barton Springs.

Where do Scottsdale residents typically land in Austin?

Scottsdale's luxury market maps most directly to Westlake Hills/Eanes ISD (top schools, affluent, hill topography, 15 min from downtown) and Lakeway (Lake Austin boat access, more space per dollar than Westlake). Old Town Scottsdale residents wanting walkable retail and restaurant culture often choose South Congress/Bouldin Creek or East Austin. Chandler/Gilbert tech corridor residents typically land Domain/Cedar Park/Round Rock.

How does Austin traffic compare to Phoenix?

Both cities are car-dependent outside their cores. Phoenix's freeway infrastructure is more extensive — Austin's I-35/183/360 interchanges can be brutal during rush hours and many Phoenix transplants are surprised by how congested Austin has become since 2018. The Domain corridor avoids MoPac/I-35 for most tech employers, which is why NW Austin and Cedar Park are popular. Central Austin's walkability (South Congress, East Austin, 2nd Street) reduces daily car dependence more than anything in Phoenix's footprint.

What about TSMC Taylor — should I wait before moving?

TSMC's Taylor, TX fab is under construction with phased openings expected through 2025–2027. If you're a TSMC Arizona engineer considering a transfer, the timing question is real — but Austin's housing market and cost basis make establishing Texas residency sooner rather than later generally advantageous.
