Tarrytown Buying Guide

Why Buyers Choose
Tarrytown

Tarrytown is one of Austin's most desirable neighborhoods for buyers who want walkability, tree-canopy streets, and direct access to Lake Austin — all within minutes of downtown. The neighborhood sits in the 78703 zip code, bounded roughly by MoPac to the east, Lake Austin to the west, Enfield Road to the south, and 35th Street to the north. For families, professionals, and anyone who values location over lot size, Tarrytown consistently ranks among the top neighborhoods in Austin. Luke Allen has helped dozens of buyers find homes in Tarrytown and knows the micro-market block by block.

Home prices in Tarrytown vary significantly depending on sub-area. South Tarrytown — closer to Enfield and Deep Eddy Pool — tends to run $900K–$1.8M for older bungalows and updated mid-century homes. Central Tarrytown between Windsor Road and West 35th sees a mix of 1950s ranches and new custom construction, typically $1.2M–$2.5M. The streets closest to Lake Austin and along Pecos Street command the highest premiums, often $2M–$4M+ for new builds and waterfront-adjacent properties. Luke Allen can walk you through current pricing in each of these micro-zones and identify where the best value sits right now.

The competitive landscape in Tarrytown is intense at certain price points. Homes priced under $1.5M — particularly updated homes near Casis Elementary — attract multiple offers and frequently sell within a week. Above $2M, the market is more balanced, with 30–60 days on market being typical. New construction and tear-down-rebuild projects are ongoing throughout the neighborhood, which means opportunities appear regularly but move fast. Common Questions

Tarrytown Homes for Sale FAQ

How much do homes cost in Tarrytown Austin?
Tarrytown home prices typically range from $800K to $4M+. Smaller bungalows and older homes on standard lots start around $800K–$1.2M. Updated mid-century and traditional homes on larger lots run $1.2M–$2.5M. New construction and estate-sized properties on premium lots near Lake Austin can exceed $3M–$4M+. Contact Luke Allen for a current pricing analysis for any Tarrytown sub-area.
What school district is Tarrytown in?
Tarrytown is zoned to Austin ISD. Elementary students attend Casis Elementary, one of the highest-rated elementary schools in the district. Middle school is O. Henry Middle School, and high school is Austin High School. The Casis Elementary zone is one of the most sought-after school assignments in central Austin and directly influences Tarrytown home values. Luke Allen can help you verify school zoning for specific addresses.
Is Tarrytown walkable?
Yes — Tarrytown is one of Austin's most walkable neighborhoods. Residents walk to Tarrytowne Shopping Center (Casis Village), Hula Hut and other Lake Austin restaurants, Deep Eddy Pool, Red Bud Isle trail, and Walsh Boat Landing. Tree-lined streets with sidewalks make it popular with families, joggers, and dog walkers. Many Tarrytown residents walk or bike to downtown Austin. Luke Allen can show you the most walkable streets and blocks.
Are there new construction homes in Tarrytown?
Yes, but they are limited and expensive. Tarrytown sees steady tear-down-and-rebuild activity where older homes on valuable lots are replaced with new custom construction. New builds in Tarrytown typically start at $2M+ and can exceed $4M for larger lots. Spec homes and custom builds appear regularly but sell quickly.
