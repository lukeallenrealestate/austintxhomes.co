Builder Insights

What the Data Means for Builders

Three reads on Q1 2026 numbers that shape underwriting decisions for the next 6-12 months of Austin new construction projects.

1. MLS-listed product moves 23 days faster

Builder-marketed inventory not on MLS averaged 96 DOM. Identical product launched on MLS by a listing agent averaged 73 DOM. On a 12-unit townhome project, that 23-day delta is roughly $48K in avoided carry on construction debt at current rates. The math: run the broker-vs-self ROI calculator with project specifics.

2. Permit pull-back favors near-completion projects

Permits fell 14.2% YoY. Completions are catching up to closings, which compresses available inventory through 2026 if the pace holds. Builders with completed or near-completed Q2 2026 product face less competition than the same product would have in Q1 2025. Strong window for spec home launches in Cedar Park, Leander, and Pflugerville where absorption is under 5 months.

3. HOME-Phase-2 product is finding price discovery

Central Austin small-lot product (78704, 78745) priced via HOME-Phase-2 mechanics is settling at $385-$520/sqft with 4-7 month absorption when MLS-launched. Earlier projects underpriced; later projects overpriced. Current pricing band is the underwriting baseline for new HOME-Phase-2 projects breaking ground in Q2-Q3 2026. Detailed Commentary

The Q1 2026 Read

Permit pull-back is real but disciplined. The 14.2% YoY decline in single-family permits is not a panic signal - it is builder discipline after a 2024-2025 absorption slowdown that left several mid-size builders carrying inventory longer than their original underwriting assumed. Builders who survived the slowdown are starting Q1 2026 with leaner pipelines and better cost-per-square-foot baselines. The data says: do not expect a 2022-style permit explosion, but do expect supply tightening through 2026 if permit pace holds.

Cedar Park, Leander, and Pflugerville are the cleanest absorption stories. All three submarkets show under 5 months of standing supply, DOM under 50 days, and stable median pricing in the $232-$262/sqft range. Buyer pool depth (school district demand plus tech-corridor commute access) makes this the highest-confidence underwriting band for the next 12 months. Spec builders with product targeting the $400K-$650K price band in these submarkets should expect MLS-launch absorption inside 60 days at current price discipline.

Central Austin price-per-foot is bifurcating. Traditional 78703 / 78731 / 78704 single-family runs $445-$580/sqft. HOME-Phase-2 small-lot product is settling lower at $385-$520/sqft - reflecting the smaller-lot, smaller-house formula and the early price discovery phase. The ceiling on small-lot product will rise as buyers absorb the value proposition (location plus new construction at a discount to traditional 78704 single-family). Builders pricing new HOME projects today should anchor underwriting to $440-$485/sqft as the conservative working assumption.

Far East suburbs (Manor, Hutto) and ultra-luxury (Bee Cave, Lakeway) are the soft spots. Manor and Hutto run 7+ months of supply at 89 median DOM, reflecting interest-rate-sensitive buyer pool concentration. Bee Cave and Lakeway run 9+ months at 118 median DOM on the $1.5M+ band, where transaction velocity is structurally lower. Builders entering these submarkets need longer absorption assumptions and price-elasticity modeling. Luxury new construction listing strategy is a different playbook than mid-market spec.

Townhome and condo permits are up 12% YoY against single-family down 14%. This is the HOME initiative impact in the data. 1,140 townhome and condo permits in Q1 2026 represents a structural shift toward higher-density product on smaller lots. Builders who have not yet evaluated HOME-Phase-2 sites should run the math now - the central Austin parcel that does not pencil as single-family often penciled as 4-6 unit small-lot under HOME. Off-market HOME-eligible parcel sourcing.

Bottom line for builders. The market is not the 2022 boom and not the 2025 trough. It is a working market where MLS launch, pricing discipline, and submarket selection separate completed projects from carrying-cost projects. The 23-day DOM delta between MLS-listed and builder-marketed is the single largest controllable variable in current project underwriting.

Frequently Asked

Austin New Construction Data FAQ

How many new construction permits were issued in Austin in Q1 2026?

The Austin-Round Rock MSA issued 4,820 single-family permits in Q1 2026, a 6.4% decline from Q4 2025 (5,150) and a 14.2% decline from Q1 2025 (5,621). Permit pull-back reflects builders pacing inventory after a soft 2025 absorption cycle. Townhome and condo permits ran 1,140 for the quarter, with HOME-Phase-2 small-lot product accounting for an estimated 18% of central Austin permits.

What is the median price per square foot for new construction in Austin?

The Austin MSA median price per square foot for closed new construction in Q1 2026 was $312. Central Austin (78704, 78703, 78731) ran $445-$580/sqft. East Austin (78702, 78722) was $385/sqft. Northwest suburbs (Cedar Park, Leander) averaged $258/sqft. South suburbs (Buda, Kyle, San Marcos) averaged $231/sqft. Per-foot pricing has held flat year-over-year as builders absorbed material cost increases through volume and design tightening rather than markup.

How long is new construction inventory taking to sell in Austin?

Q1 2026 absorption was 5.8 months of supply across the MSA, up from 4.9 in Q1 2025. Spec inventory listed at MLS launch absorbed in 73 median days on market vs 96 days for builder-marketed inventory not on MLS. Townhome projects with broker MLS listings absorbed 31% faster than developer-marketed identical product, validating the MLS-launch advantage that drives the listing partnership conversation.

Which Austin submarkets are strongest for new construction sell-through?

Cedar Park (3.9 months) and Leander (4.4 months) lead on absorption, driven by school district demand and a stabilized buyer pool. Pflugerville (4.7 months) is third. Central Austin (78704, 78745) runs 5.2 months on a higher price band. Far East suburbs (Manor, Hutto) lag at 7.1+ months as buyer financing sensitivity to rates concentrates in lower-price submarkets. Bee Cave and Lakeway (luxury new construction) run 8-10 months at the $1.5M+ band.

Has Austin new construction recovered from the 2024-2025 correction?

Partial recovery. Permits are 14.2% below Q1 2025, indicating builder caution. Closings improved 8.1% over Q4 2025 as buyer activity unfroze post-spring rate cuts. Months of supply increased modestly because permit pull-back lags closing improvement. The data picture: builders are not pulling new permits at 2022 pace, but existing inventory is moving faster. Net effect through 2026: tightening supply if permit pace stays disciplined, which favors developers with completed or near-completed product.

What does the HOME initiative mean for Austin new construction data?

HOME Phase 1 (effective late 2023) allows up to 3 units on what was previously single-family lots. HOME Phase 2 (effective 2024) reduced minimum lot size to 1,800 sqft, introduced Site Plan Lite for 4-16 unit projects, and Infill Plat for streamlined small-lot subdivision. Q1 2026 permit data shows ~870 HOME-eligible new permits in central Austin (representing the early adopter wave). Builder pricing on HOME-Phase-2 small-lot product has stabilized at $385-$520/sqft in 78704 and 78745, with 4-7 month absorption when listed via MLS partnership.
