The Community

What Makes Driftwood Different

Driftwood is an unincorporated community in Hays County, tucked into the cedar and limestone Hill Country southwest of Austin. It occupies a rare position in the Austin market: genuinely rural character and large estate lots, yet close enough to Austin's tech and finance corridors that serious professionals actually live here full time.

The community developed around a reverence for the land itself — the live oaks, the creek beds, the escarpment views, the clear Hill Country night sky. Properties here aren't measured in square footage of floor plan; they're measured in acres and privacy and what the land feels like when you step outside. That's a different buyer than you find in any Austin suburb.

Salt Lick BBQ has operated here since 1967 and remains something of a community landmark — an outdoor pit barbecue institution with a BYOB pavilion that hosts everyone from UT students to Fortune 500 executives. It's also a reflection of what Driftwood is: unpretentious by disposition, excellent by standards.

Distance and Access

Driftwood sits approximately 25 miles southwest of downtown Austin via FM 1826 or FM 150 through Dripping Springs. The drive is typically 35–45 minutes, depending on route and time of day. The Mopac/SH 45 toll corridor and the Dripping Springs US 290 connection make the commute realistic for most Austin employers.

Why People Choose Driftwood
  • Large lot estates (2–50+ acres) at prices that don't exist this close to Austin anywhere else
  • Driftwood Golf & Ranch Club membership — championship golf, ranch amenities, and a members-only social community
  • Access to the Driftwood Downtown Social Club — the city presence that lets members enjoy Austin's urban core while living on the land
  • Dripping Springs ISD schools — consistently among the highest-rated districts in Texas
  • Dark sky community — minimal light pollution, genuine Hill Country night sky, something the suburbs simply cannot offer
  • Wineries and breweries within minutes: Treaty Oak Distilling, Jester King Brewery, Duchman Family Winery, and Driftwood Estate Winery are all neighbors
  • No HOA in most areas — property owners maintain genuine freedom in how they use and develop their land
  • Permanently protected land adjacent to many properties through conservation easements and the Barton Creek watershed preservation zone
Private Clubs & Lifestyle

The Clubs That Define Driftwood Living

Driftwood's private club infrastructure is what separates it from every other rural community within range of Austin. Two distinct memberships — one rooted in the land, one in the city.

⛳

Driftwood Golf & Ranch Club

A private members-only club set on Hill Country acreage featuring a championship golf course, ranch-style event facilities, and a community of estate home owners who share a reverence for land and a commitment to privacy. The Golf & Ranch Club defines the aspirational center of Driftwood's luxury market — properties near or within the club boundary command the strongest premiums in the area.

🏙

Driftwood Downtown Social Club

The urban complement to ranch living. The Driftwood Downtown Social Club gives members a curated presence in Austin's city core — a gathering space for the Driftwood community when they're in town for business, events, or simply to access the city. It bridges the gap that has historically made full-time rural living difficult for Austin professionals who need to remain connected to the city's social and business fabric.

What You'll Find Here

Driftwood Property Types & Neighborhoods

Driftwood isn't a subdivision — it's a community. Property types range from intimate 2-acre ranchettes to full ranch estates of 50+ acres.

Golf & Ranch Club Estates

Properties within the Driftwood Golf & Ranch Club community. Premium pricing, golf course access, and a curated neighbor set of like-minded estate owners.

Hill Country Ranchettes

2–20 acre tracts with custom homes, cedar and live oak canopy, limestone outcroppings, and the classic Hill Country setting. The heart of what makes Driftwood desirable.

Creek & Waterfront Properties

Properties fronting Onion Creek, Barton Creek tributaries, or seasonal Hill Country draws. Creek access and swimming holes are among the most sought-after features in the market.

Working Ranch Properties

Larger acreage with agricultural exemption, livestock, and working operations. These properties offer significant property tax advantages and attract a specific buyer profile.

New Construction Estates

Custom builds on large lots by Hill Country architects and builders. Driftwood attracts builders who specialize in the indoor-outdoor Hill Country aesthetic — expansive porches, natural stone, and site-specific design.

Vineyard & Winery Adjacent

Properties near the Driftwood wine corridor — Duchman Family Winery, Driftwood Estate Winery, and neighboring vineyards. Some properties include their own small vineyard or orchard operations.

Questions

Frequently Asked About Driftwood TX

What is the Driftwood Golf & Ranch Club?

The Driftwood Golf & Ranch Club is a private members-only club in Driftwood, TX featuring a championship golf course, ranch-style amenities, and a community of estate homes set on large Hill Country lots. Membership provides access to golf, social events, and a private community lifestyle approximately 25 miles from downtown Austin.

What is the price range for homes in Driftwood TX?

Driftwood home prices range from approximately $800K for smaller ranchette properties to $4M+ for larger custom estates with significant acreage. Golf & Ranch Club properties and homes with Hill Country views command the strongest premiums. Lot size is a primary value driver — properties of 2–10+ acres are common.

How far is Driftwood TX from downtown Austin?

Driftwood is approximately 25–30 miles southwest of downtown Austin, a 35–45 minute drive under normal traffic conditions. The area is accessed primarily via FM 1826, FM 150, and the Dripping Springs corridor on US 290.

What schools serve Driftwood TX?

Driftwood is served by the Hays Consolidated Independent School District (Hays CISD), with many areas zoned to Dripping Springs ISD. Dripping Springs High School is consistently ranked among the top high schools in Texas and is a significant driver for families choosing this corridor.

Is Driftwood TX a good investment?

Driftwood has appreciated significantly over the past decade as Austin's growth has pushed demand into the Hill Country corridor. Large-lot estate properties near the Golf & Ranch Club have seen consistent value increases driven by limited supply, strong school zoning, and the irreplaceable quality of the Hill Country setting. It is widely regarded as one of the stronger long-term holds in the greater Austin area.
