The School District

Why Families Choose
Eanes ISD

Eanes ISD is consistently ranked the best public school district in Texas — and it has been for over a decade. When families relocating to Austin ask about schools, Eanes is the first name that comes up, and usually the last one they need to hear.

The district serves Westlake Hills, Rollingwood, and surrounding unincorporated areas of Travis County just west of Austin. It is geographically small — roughly 8 square miles — which means the community is tight-knit, the schools are well-funded through high property tax revenue, and the student-to-teacher ratios stay lower than comparably rated suburban districts.

Westlake High School, the district's sole high school, consistently places in the top 100 public high schools nationally. AP enrollment is among the highest in the state. College placement rates are exceptional. And the athletics program is strong enough that Westlake football has won multiple state championships without the district feeling like it sacrifices academics for sports.

For buyers, Eanes ISD homes carry a real premium — typically 20–35% above comparable properties just outside the district boundary. That premium holds. Families who buy in Eanes tend to stay until their youngest graduates. Turnover is low, inventory is limited, and demand is consistent.

Neighborhoods Within the District

  • Westlake Hills
    The primary city within Eanes ISD. Wooded lots, Hill Country views, and a mix of 1970s ranches and new custom builds. Most homes are $1.1M–$2.5M.
  • Rollingwood
    Small, walkable enclave adjacent to Barton Creek Greenbelt. Tightly held — rarely more than 5–10 homes on market at any time. $1M–$2M range.
  • Barton Creek
    Master-planned community around the Barton Creek Resort & Spa. Golf course lots, gated sections, Hill Country terrain. $1.5M–$4M+.
  • Rob Roy
    Established neighborhood along Loop 360 with large lots and mature trees. Good value relative to Barton Creek. $900K–$1.8M.
  • Lost Creek
    Family-focused neighborhood with large yards and good proximity to Westwood Middle School. Entry point for Eanes ISD buyers. $850K–$1.4M.

Common Questions

Eanes ISD FAQ

What cities and neighborhoods are in Eanes ISD?
Eanes ISD serves Westlake Hills, Rollingwood, and portions of unincorporated Travis County west of Austin. The primary zip code is 78746. Neighborhoods include Barton Creek, Westwood Estates, Rob Roy, Lost Creek, and several gated communities along Lake Austin. The district does not serve any part of Austin proper.
What are the schools in Eanes ISD?
Eanes ISD has five elementary schools (Barton Creek, Bridge Point, Cedar Creek, Eanes, and Valley View), two middle schools (Hill Country and West Ridge), and one high school — Westlake High. Westlake High is consistently ranked among the top public high schools in Texas, with high AP enrollment and strong college placement rates.
What is the typical home price range in Eanes ISD?
Homes in Eanes ISD typically range from $850K to $4M+. Entry-level single-family homes start around $850K–$1.1M for older ranches. Newer construction and larger lots typically run $1.5M–$3.5M. The Barton Creek area and gated communities top out above $4M. Lakefront properties along Lake Austin are the district's highest-priced tier.
Is Eanes ISD really the best school district in Texas?
Eanes ISD consistently ranks #1 or #2 in Texas by multiple measures — TEA accountability ratings, AP exam pass rates, SAT/ACT scores, and college enrollment. Westlake High School regularly places in national top-100 lists. The district benefits from extremely high property tax revenue due to home values and an engaged parent community that directly funds programs and facilities.
How competitive is the Eanes ISD real estate market?
Very competitive. Well-priced homes in the $900K–$1.5M range typically receive multiple offers within the first week. Inventory is constrained because the district is geographically small and homeowner turnover is lower than other Austin submarkets. Having a pre-approval and an agent who knows the micro-market is essential.
Can I commute to downtown Austin from Westlake Hills?
Yes. Westlake Hills is 8–12 miles from downtown Austin. Most commutes are 15–25 minutes outside peak hours via MoPac or Loop 360. Rush-hour traffic on MoPac can extend to 35–45 minutes. Many Eanes ISD residents also commute north to the Domain tech hub (Apple, Amazon, Meta, Indeed) via Loop 360, typically 20–30 minutes.
