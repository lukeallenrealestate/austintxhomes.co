Buying land here

Land along the Pedernales is not turnkey.

Buying land is materially different from buying a built home. The price is lower, but the work is heavier. You are signing up for: site planning, well drilling and verification, septic system design and permitting, FEMA floodplain analysis, county building code review, electrical service installation, road and driveway construction, and either custom build management or pre-fab placement.

For experienced Hill Country builders this is straightforward. For first-time land buyers from Austin it can be a learning curve. The best path forward is to talk through the build sequence with someone who has done it on the river before, ideally before you commit to a specific parcel.

Pedernales land prices vary widely. Spicewood and Briarcliff subdivision lots with city water and sewer hookup, paved access, and existing utility runs typically price $400K to $1M for 1 to 5 acres. Larger off-grid acreage in the Johnson City corridor can be found from $300K for 10 to 25 acres without river frontage, scaling up to $2M+ for 50+ acre tracts with river frontage and ranch infrastructure.

For raw river-frontage land specifically, expect $400K to $1.5M for 5 to 25 acres depending on frontage quality, location, and county. The same money buys very different land profiles in Travis County (smaller, closer to Austin) versus Burnet County (larger, more rural).

What to verify

Pre-offer diligence on Pedernales land.

01

Well drilling potential

Adjacent properties give a strong indication of well depth and yield. A neighbor who hit good water at 200 feet probably means you will too. A neighbor who drilled three dry holes is a red flag.

02

Septic system feasibility

Soil percolation testing determines whether a conventional septic field will work or whether you need an aerobic system. Aerobic systems cost meaningfully more to install and maintain.

03

Building envelope and access

Where can the house actually go? FEMA floodplain, county setback rules, slope, tree clearing, driveway grading, and turnaround requirements all constrain the buildable footprint.

04

Utility runs and cost

Electric service from the nearest meter to your build site. Internet (Starlink is now standard for rural Hill Country builds, but verify). Propane delivery access. Each utility run has a cost.

Most river-frontage parcels along the Pedernales have some portion in FEMA-designated floodplain. The 100-year and 500-year floodplain footprints typically extend 50 to 300 feet from the river edge. Building inside the floodplain is restricted by county and FEMA rules. The buildable envelope on a river-frontage parcel is the area outside the floodplain. Always pull the FEMA floodplain map for any river-frontage parcel before offering.
Most parcels have electric service available at or near the property line. Water is almost always private well, with city water available only in pockets near Spicewood and Briarcliff. Sewer is septic system, no exceptions outside city limits. Internet is typically Starlink, with some properties having access to fiber. Natural gas is rare; propane is standard.
