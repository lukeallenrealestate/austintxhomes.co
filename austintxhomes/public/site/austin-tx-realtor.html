Common Questions

Luke Allen — Austin Realtor FAQ

Do I have to pay Luke Allen to be my buyer's agent in Austin?
No. Buyer representation with Luke Allen is free to you — the seller pays the buyer's agent commission. As of August 2024, you must sign a Buyer Representation Agreement before touring, but that agreement simply formalizes the relationship. You pay nothing out of pocket to work with Luke.
How do I verify that Luke Allen is a licensed Austin Realtor?
Every Texas real estate agent must be licensed by the Texas Real Estate Commission (TREC). You can verify Luke Allen's license at trec.texas.gov — his license number is 788149. Search it directly to confirm Luke is an active, licensed Texas Realtor.
What's the difference between a Realtor and a real estate agent in Texas?
All Realtors are licensed real estate agents, but not all agents are Realtors. A Realtor is a member of the National Association of Realtors and agrees to follow its Code of Ethics. Practically, the main thing that matters is whether your agent is licensed, local, and works primarily in the areas you're targeting.
How long does it take to buy a home in Austin?
From pre-approval to closing typically takes 45–75 days. Finding the right home can take 2 weeks to 3 months depending on your criteria and how fast you can move when something good comes up. Austin neighborhoods like South Congress and Crestview move fast — sometimes same-day offers on the right listing.
How much does it cost to sell a home in Austin?
Seller costs typically include: agent commission (negotiated at time of listing), title insurance (~0.5–0.7% of sale price), prorated property taxes, any agreed repairs from inspection, and closing costs (~1–2%). Luke Allen is upfront about all fees before you sign anything.
Do I need a local Austin Realtor or can I use an agent from out of town?
You can use any licensed Texas agent, but local knowledge matters enormously. Austin's neighborhoods have wildly different flood risk, traffic patterns, school district lines, and price dynamics block by block. An agent who knows Crestview from Cherrywood — or why 78704 trades at a premium to 78745 — will serve you far better.
