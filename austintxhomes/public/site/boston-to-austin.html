9%

The Massachusetts Millionaires Surtax — Effective 2023

Massachusetts voters approved a 4% surtax on income over $1,000,000 in 2022, effective January 2023. Combined with the 5% base rate, Massachusetts now taxes high earners at 9% on income above $1M. This applies to salary, bonuses, RSU vests, capital gains, and business sale proceeds. A biotech executive with a $2M compensation year pays ~$95,000 in Massachusetts state income tax. In Texas: $0.

Even below the $1M threshold, Massachusetts's 5% flat tax is a permanent, annual cost that Texas eliminates entirely. The savings are straightforward: every dollar of income, at every level, is taxed at 5% in Massachusetts and 0% in Texas.

More meaningfully: Boston's winters are operationally disruptive — storm preparation, snow removal, heating costs, and the occasional nor'easter that shuts down the city for days. Austin's winters are a non-event. Most Boston transplants describe their first Austin January as something between relief and disbelief.

The summer trade. Austin's July averages 97°F — meaningfully hotter than Boston's 82°F July. But Boston's summer humidity (75–80%) and Austin's summer humidity (45–55%) make Boston's summer feel hotter than the numbers suggest. Most Boston transplants find Austin summers intense but manageable with AC, and describe the winters as the clear winner. Plan for $180–$280/month in electric bills May–October.

For Families

Austin Schools
for Boston Families

Boston / Newton Context

Newton Public Schools and Brookline Public Schools are among Massachusetts's strongest — and that's saying something. Boston Public Schools are highly variable. Cambridge Rindge and Latin is strong. Massachusetts families tend to have high school expectations; Austin requires the same research-by-campus approach as Boston's inconsistent BPS system.

Austin ISD — Central

AISD is highly variable by campus — research by specific address. Strong elementaries: Bryker Woods, Casis, Barton Hills, Travis Heights. Strong high schools: McCallum (Hyde Park) and Bowie (South). The Hyde Park and South Austin AISD corridors compare reasonably to strong Boston Public Schools campuses. Avoid relying on district-level ratings.

Eanes ISD — Westlake

The Austin equivalent of Newton Public Schools in reputation and consistency. Westlake HS is in Texas's top 5. Located in Westlake Hills, 15–20 min from downtown. Homes $900K–$2M+. The right option for Newton and Brookline families with non-negotiable school district expectations who are comfortable at that price point.

Private Schools

Central Austin has strong private options for families who want Boston-caliber education regardless of zip code: St. Andrew's Episcopal, St. Michael's Catholic Academy, Regents School of Austin, and Austin Waldorf. Tuition runs $18,000–$30,000/year — meaningful but affordable at Boston biotech and tech comp levels, and worth factoring into the housing-vs-school calculation.

How much do I save on taxes moving from Boston to Austin?

Massachusetts has a 5% flat income tax on all income. Texas has zero. On $200K income you save ~$10,000/year. On $400K you save ~$20,000/year. Massachusetts added a 4% surtax on income over $1M in 2023 — making the effective top rate 9%. A biotech executive with $1.5M in income saves ~$70,000+/year. These savings are permanent and immediate from your first Texas paycheck.

What is the Massachusetts millionaires tax?

Massachusetts voters approved a 4% surtax on income above $1,000,000 in 2022, effective January 2023. Combined with the 5% base rate, Massachusetts now taxes income over $1M at 9%. This applies to salary, bonuses, RSU vests, capital gains, and business sale proceeds. For founders approaching a liquidity event, establishing Texas residency before — not after — the event is especially important. Consult a CPA on timing.

What Austin neighborhood is most like Boston's South End or Cambridge?

Boston's South End maps closely to Bouldin Creek and South Congress — walkable, upscale but lived-in, locally-owned everything. Cambridge and Somerville translate to Hyde Park and Rosedale — academic community feel, strong neighborhood identity, excellent schools nearby. Beacon Hill buyers typically land in Clarksville or Tarrytown. Jamaica Plain transplants usually find East Austin feels most like home.

How does Boston housing compare to Austin?

Boston and Cambridge are among America's most expensive housing markets. South End and Back Bay 2BR condos run $900K–$1.5M. Cambridge 3BR homes run $1.0M–$1.8M. Austin's comparable neighborhoods — Bouldin Creek, Hyde Park, Barton Hills — run $550K–$1.1M. Texas property taxes are higher (1.8–2.4% vs. MA ~1.1%), but on a $750K Austin home vs. a $1.4M Cambridge home, the absolute bills are similar — and the income tax savings are substantial.

Is Austin a good move for Boston biotech or pharma professionals?

Austin's life sciences sector is growing but not Kendall Square. Dell Medical School at UT, biotech startups, and remote-friendly roles give Boston biotech professionals options. Many make the move for lifestyle and the Massachusetts millionaires surtax savings while working remotely for Boston-based companies. For those requiring Kendall Square's specific cluster, Boston remains the center — but for senior leaders and founders, the Texas tax picture is transformational.

How are Austin winters compared to Boston?

Dramatically different. Boston averages 48 inches of snow per year, January highs of 36°F, and regular nor'easters. Austin's January average high is 61°F with rare freezing days. Austin gets ~300 sunny days per year; Boston gets ~200. Most Boston transplants describe their first Austin January as revelatory — mild, sunny, and completely operational, while their former Boston colleagues are dealing with storm prep and shoveling.
