The Mortgage Question

Qualifying for a Mortgage
After Divorce

The biggest hurdle for most post-divorce buyers isn't finding the right home — it's qualifying for a mortgage on a single income. Here's what changes and how to navigate it.

Your Income Picture Has Changed

You now qualify on your income alone. If you were previously qualifying on two incomes, your maximum loan amount will be lower. Child support received can be counted as income if it will continue for 3+ more years (documentation required). Alimony received also counts with documentation. Child support or alimony paid reduces your qualifying income.

Joint Debt Is Still Your Debt

If the marital home mortgage is still in your name — even if your ex is paying it under the decree — lenders count it against your debt-to-income ratio until it's refinanced out of your name or paid off. This is one of the most common post-divorce mortgage surprises. Get the marital home mortgage resolved before applying for your new loan.

Equity as Your Down Payment

Your equity from the marital home sale is your down payment. Austin homes at $550K–$900K with 10–20% down require $55K–$180K. Most divorcing couples coming out of appreciated Austin homes have sufficient equity — the question is timeline. The marital home sale should close before you close on your new purchase.

What Lenders Need from the Decree

Expect your lender to ask for: the full divorce decree, documentation of child support or alimony, proof the marital home mortgage is being paid by the other spouse (12 months of cancelled checks if it's in your name), and the deed transfer if applicable. Work with a lender experienced in post-divorce buyers — the paperwork requirements are specific.

Before you start looking: Get pre-approved by a lender experienced with post-divorce buyers. Pre-approval is your foundation — it tells you your real budget and gives you credibility with sellers in Austin's competitive market. The Buying Process

How to Buy After Divorce
Step by Step

Before You Start Looking

  • Wait until the divorce decree is finalized — purchasing during divorce creates community property complications
  • Get pre-approved with a lender experienced in post-divorce borrowers — before any home search
  • Confirm the marital home mortgage is out of your name (or that lender will account for it correctly) before applying
  • Document child support / alimony for at least 3 months before the lender application if it's part of your qualifying income
  • Check your credit — joint accounts may have issues from the divorce transition period; resolve any errors
  • Know your down payment source — marital home equity proceeds must typically be "seasoned" (in your account 60+ days) before closing

During the Search

  • Move quickly on well-priced homes in Hyde Park and East Austin — they often go under contract in days
  • Consider school district carefully if you have kids — address matters, not just the neighborhood name
  • Think about maintenance — a smaller, lower-maintenance home is often the right call for the first post-divorce purchase
  • Be honest about your commute — without the compromise that comes with a partner's commute, pick the location that genuinely works for your life

At Contract and Closing

  • Your new purchase is separate property — you are the only buyer, the only signer. No complications from your former spouse.
  • File for the homestead exemption after January 1 of the first full year of ownership — saves $500–$1,000+ per year on your Austin property tax bill
  • Update your will and beneficiary designations — the home should reflect your current wishes immediately after purchase
  • Update your homeowner's insurance — separate from your former spouse's policy
