Market Positioning

Austin's multifamily market spans from entry-level duplexes in suburban corridors to premium multi-unit buildings in central Austin. The sweet spot for first-time multifamily investors is the $400K–$750K range, where you can find well-maintained duplexes with strong rental histories.

Properties above $1M are typically larger multi-unit buildings, newer construction duplexes in premium neighborhoods (78704, 78702), or value-add opportunities with significant upside.

Common Questions

Multifamily Investment FAQ

Is Austin a good market for multifamily investment in 2026?
Austin remains one of the strongest multifamily markets in Texas. Population growth exceeds 100 people per day, rental demand is high across all unit types, and small multifamily (2-4 units) offers favorable financing with conventional loans. Cap rates on duplexes and triplexes in central Austin range from 4-6%, with higher yields in east Austin and suburban corridors.
What is the average price of a duplex in Austin?
Duplex prices vary significantly by location. Central Austin (78704, 78702, 78722) typically lists between $600K-$1.2M. East Austin and northeast corridors run $400K-$700K. Suburban duplexes in Pflugerville, Round Rock, and Kyle can be found starting around $350K-$500K.
How long do multifamily properties take to sell?
Multifamily properties typically spend 30-60 days on market, longer than single-family homes. Well-priced duplexes in desirable neighborhoods can sell in under 2 weeks. Larger buildings often take 60-120 days due to the smaller buyer pool and more complex underwriting.
Do I need a commercial loan for a duplex?
No — 1-4 unit properties qualify for conventional residential financing. If you live in one unit (house hacking), you can use FHA with 3.5% down or VA with 0% down. Investment-only purchases typically require 20-25% down with conventional loans.
What are the best Austin neighborhoods for multifamily?
East Austin (78702, 78722) offers the best appreciation potential. South Austin (78704, 78745) has strong rental demand near SoCo and Barton Springs. North Austin near the Domain has high occupancy from tech workers. For value plays, look at 78753, 78758, and Del Valle — lower entry points with improving infrastructure.
How is cap rate calculated for Austin multifamily?
Cap rate = Net Operating Income / Purchase Price. For a duplex generating $3,500/mo gross rent with $1,200/mo in expenses (taxes, insurance, maintenance, vacancy), NOI is $27,600/year. At a $550K purchase price, cap rate is 5.0%. I can run detailed cap rate analysis on any listing — just ask.
