Equity and Vesting

Meta RSUs in Texas:
The Full Tax Picture

💰
State Income Tax: Zero

Texas has no state income tax. California's top rate is 13.3%. On a Meta senior engineer vest of $200K in RSUs, you keep $26,000 more in Texas than in California. On a $400K vest year, you keep $50,000+ more. This compounds across your entire Meta tenure.

📈
Capital Gains: Texas Advantage

California taxes long-term capital gains as ordinary income — up to 13.3%. Texas has no capital gains tax at the state level. If you hold Meta shares after vest, your appreciation is taxed at federal rates only. For senior employees with significant equity, this is meaningful over a career.

🏠
Property Tax Reality

Texas property taxes run 1.8%–2.4% of assessed value — higher than California's Prop 13-capped rates. On a $500K Cedar Park home: ~$9,000–$12,000/year. This partially offsets income tax savings. But even accounting for property taxes, the net financial benefit of Texas vs. California is strongly positive at Meta compensation levels.

Common Questions

Meta Austin
Relocation FAQ

Where do most Meta Austin employees live?

The northwest Austin corridor is dominant: Cedar Park and Leander for families (Leander ISD + fast 183A commute), Great Hills and Balcones for established-neighborhood buyers, Domain/Arboretum for singles and couples. Lakeway attracts senior employees with larger budgets who want the Hill Country lifestyle. Unlike Menlo Park where the campus is surrounded by $3M+ homes, Austin gives Meta employees genuine neighborhood choices across a wide price range.

How much will I save on taxes moving from California to Texas for Meta?

At $200K total comp: roughly $18,000–$20,000/year in state income tax savings. At $350K: $38,000–$46,000/year. At $500K: $60,000–$65,000/year. These are state income tax savings only — federal taxes are unchanged. Also consider: California taxes capital gains as ordinary income at up to 13.3%; Texas has no state capital gains tax. Consult a CPA for your specific numbers.

How long is the commute from Austin suburbs to Meta's offices?

Domain/Arboretum: 5–12 min. Great Hills: 8–15 min. Cedar Park: 15–22 min via 183A. Leander: 20–30 min via 183A. Central Austin/Zilker: 15–25 min via MoPac south. Lakeway: 25–38 min. Round Rock: 22–35 min via US-183 north. Avoid South Austin — wrong direction, 40–60 min against PM traffic.

Does Meta offer relocation assistance for Austin moves?

Meta offers relocation packages for qualifying hires and transfers. Packages typically include a lump sum or managed move option. For transfers, packages tend to be more comprehensive. Confirm specifics with Meta HR. If you have temporary housing covered, use that time to rent in Cedar Park or the Domain and experience the corridor before buying.

Is Austin housing as expensive as it was during the 2021–2022 boom?

No. Austin prices peaked in early 2022 and corrected 15–20% from peak through 2023. Northwest Austin (Cedar Park, Leander, Great Hills) is stable and appreciating moderately. This is a mature tech-employee corridor with strong school districts — not a speculative market. Coming from Bay Area prices, Austin at any point in the last decade looks extraordinary.

How do Austin schools compare to schools in Menlo Park or Palo Alto?

Leander ISD and Lake Travis ISD are strong districts that compete with good suburban California districts. Eanes ISD (Westlake Hills) is the Austin-area equivalent of a top Bay Area public school district — Westlake HS consistently ranks in Texas's top 5. The key difference: Texas districts have more within-district variability, so address-level research matters more than in uniformly high-performing Palo Alto Unified.
