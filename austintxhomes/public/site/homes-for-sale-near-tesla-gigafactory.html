Tesla Relocation Buying Guide

Buying a Home Near
Tesla Giga Texas

Tesla Giga Texas opened in April 2022 at 13101 Harold Green Rd in Del Valle — and it fundamentally changed the southeast Austin housing market. When you are searching for homes for sale near Tesla Gigafactory, the first decision is not about price range. It is about which commute corridor fits your lifestyle: Del Valle for maximum proximity, SE Austin for urban walkability, or Manor for space and value. Luke Allen works this corridor daily and can walk you through what any active listing is actually worth before you tour it.

Del Valle (78617) is the top choice for Tesla employees who want the shortest possible drive. Homes here are predominantly 2019–2025 new construction on 5,000–8,000 sq ft lots. The price-to-size ratio is the best in the Austin metro — a 4-bedroom, 2,200 sq ft home can be found for $350K–$450K. The trade-off: Del Valle is light on retail and dining compared to central Austin. Most residents drive to Bastrop, SE Austin, or the Domain for major shopping. For Tesla shift workers or buyers who spend most of their time at home or at work, Del Valle is an easy call. Explore the full Moving to Austin guide for broader context on Austin living.

SE Austin (78744) is the pick for Tesla employees who want a short commute but also want access to South Congress, East Austin's restaurant scene, and Austin's urban core on days off. The 78744 zip code runs from McKinney Falls State Park in the southeast to Slaughter Lane in the north. Homes here are a mix of 1970s–1990s builds and newer construction. Average prices are $50K–$100K higher than Del Valle, but you gain Austin ISD school assignments and faster access to downtown. See current listings on the Austin buyer's page for the full metro picture.

Manor (78653) is the value play for buyers who want more land per dollar. Half-acre lots are common under $400K — something nearly impossible to find anywhere closer to Austin's urban core. Manor ISD is smaller and improving. The commute to Giga Texas runs 20–25 minutes on SH-130 — fast and uncongested compared to I-35. For Tesla employees relocating from markets like California or the Pacific Northwest where homes near major employers cost $800K+, Manor and Del Valle offer a staggering cost-of-living improvement. Pair the Texas residency guide with your relocation planning for broader financial context.

Del Valle ISD boundaries are still shifting as the district adds capacity. Some Del Valle addresses fall outside the district's newer school boundaries. What neighborhoods are closest to Tesla Gigafactory Austin?
Tesla Giga Texas is at 13101 Harold Green Rd, Del Valle TX 78617 — off SH-130, southeast of Austin. The closest residential areas are Del Valle (78617), SE Austin (78744), and Manor (78653). Del Valle is under 10 minutes via SH-130. SE Austin is 15–20 minutes. Manor is 20–25 minutes. Luke Allen can map any specific address against the exact commute time to Giga Texas.
How much do homes cost near Tesla Gigafactory Austin?
Del Valle (78617) single-family homes run $280K–$550K. SE Austin (78744) ranges from $320K–$650K. Manor (78653) starts around $290K. New construction communities in the area often start in the low $300Ks. Prices have appreciated since Giga Texas opened in 2022 but remain well below the Austin metro median.
What is the commute from Del Valle to Tesla Gigafactory?
From Del Valle (78617), the commute to Giga Texas is typically 5–12 minutes via SH-130 or Harold Green Rd. From SE Austin (78744), expect 15–20 minutes via SH-130 South. From Manor (78653), 20–25 minutes via SH-130 North. SH-130 has minimal congestion compared to I-35 and is a reliable commute corridor even during peak hours.
