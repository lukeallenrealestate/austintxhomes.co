The School District

Why Families Choose
Hays CISD

Hays Consolidated ISD is where affordability meets growth in the Austin metro. For families who want to own a home — not just rent — without stretching past their budget, Hays CISD is the most realistic path into the market. Kyle and Buda consistently rank among the fastest-growing cities in Texas, and the school district is expanding right alongside them.

The district serves Kyle, Buda, parts of San Marcos, and portions of the Wimberley and Driftwood areas. It covers a wide swath of Hays County south of Austin along the I-35 corridor — a geography that ranges from dense new-construction subdivisions to Hill Country acreage on the western edge.

Hays CISD is TEA B-rated and improving. The district has invested heavily in new campuses, technology infrastructure, and teacher recruitment as enrollment has surged. Four high schools now serve the district, with Johnson HS in Buda being one of the newest additions. Class sizes in newer campuses are competitive with higher-rated suburban districts.

The real draw for buyers is value. A new-construction 4-bedroom home in Hays CISD costs $300K–$400K — the same home would run $500K–$600K in Round Rock ISD or $900K+ in Eanes ISD. For first-time buyers and investors, the math is straightforward. And with Texas State University in nearby San Marcos, the area has a built-in economic anchor beyond Austin commuter demand.

If affordability is your priority without leaving the Austin metro, Hays CISD is the district to explore. Browse listings below, check out homes under $400K, or reach out to talk through the market.

Market Reality

What Buying in Hays CISD
Actually Looks Like

The Hays CISD market is builder-driven. Unlike established Austin neighborhoods where resale homes dominate, Kyle and Buda are defined by new construction. Builders like Meritage, Lennar, D.R. Horton, and Taylor Morrison are all active here, and they are competing on price. That competition benefits buyers — especially first-time buyers who can leverage builder incentives like rate buydowns, closing cost credits, and design upgrades.

At the $250K–$375K entry level in central Kyle, you are looking at older inventory — 2005–2015 builds, smaller lots, and functional but not updated finishes. These homes sell quickly because they are some of the most affordable single-family options in the entire Austin metro. If you are budget-focused, this is where you start.

The $300K–$450K sweet spot in Plum Creek, Anthem, and Sunfield is where most activity happens. New-construction 3–4 bedroom homes with modern floor plans, community amenities, and solid school assignments. Days on market vary — builder inventory can sit if it is overpriced, but well-priced resale homes still move in 2–3 weeks.

Above $500K, you are moving into premium Buda locations or the Driftwood/Wimberley edge where acreage lots and custom homes are the draw. This segment is less competitive and more negotiable. Sellers here need to price correctly or expect longer days on market.

The I-35 corridor south of Austin is where the region's growth is concentrating. Population projections for Kyle and Buda are aggressive, and home values have appreciated steadily even as the broader Austin market softened. For investors and long-term buyers, the fundamentals are strong. Start your home search or talk to me about what is available right now.

Common Questions

Hays CISD FAQ

What cities and neighborhoods are in Hays CISD?
Hays CISD serves Kyle, Buda, partial San Marcos, and portions of Wimberley and Driftwood. Primary zip codes are 78640 (Kyle), 78610 (Buda), 78666 (San Marcos), and 78676 (Wimberley). Major neighborhoods include Plum Creek, Anthem, Sunfield, Garlic Creek, Shadow Creek, and Hillside Terrace. The district is one of the fastest-growing in Texas, with new master-planned communities breaking ground regularly.
What are the schools in Hays CISD?
Hays CISD has four high schools — Hays HS, Lehman HS, Jack C. Hays HS, and Johnson HS. Middle schools include Chapa, Barton, Dahlstrom, and Wallace. Notable elementary campuses include Negley, Tobias, Fuentes, Elm Grove, and Science Hall. The district is TEA B-rated and continues to add new campuses as enrollment grows rapidly.
What is the typical home price range in Hays CISD?
Homes in Hays CISD typically range from $250K to $650K, making it the most affordable major school district in the Austin metro. Entry-level homes in Central Kyle start around $250K–$375K. Master-planned communities like Plum Creek and Anthem run $300K–$450K. Buda neighborhoods range $325K–$550K. Driftwood and Wimberley-edge properties with acreage top out at $450K–$650K+.
Is Hays CISD a good school district?
Hays CISD is TEA B-rated and rapidly improving. As one of Texas's fastest-expanding districts, it has invested heavily in new campuses, technology, and staffing. While it does not carry the same rankings as Eanes or Lake Travis ISD, the district offers solid academics at a fraction of the housing cost. For families prioritizing affordability and new construction, Hays CISD delivers strong value relative to price.
How is the commute from Kyle or Buda to downtown Austin?
Kyle and Buda are 30–45 minutes from downtown Austin via I-35, depending on traffic and time of day. Rush hour can push commute times toward 50–60 minutes. San Marcos is roughly 15–20 minutes south of Kyle. Many Hays CISD residents work remotely or commute to south Austin employers along the I-35 corridor. The I-35 expansion project is expected to improve travel times over the coming years.
Why are so many people moving to Kyle and Buda?
Kyle and Buda consistently rank among the fastest-growing cities in Texas. The primary drivers are affordability — you can buy a new-construction home for $300K–$400K that would cost $500K+ in central Austin — plus proximity to Austin jobs, new master-planned communities with amenities, and the expanding I-35 corridor. Builder incentives including rate buydowns and closing cost credits make entry even more accessible.
