Seattle vs. Austin:
The Honest Comparison

Amazon is Austin's most Seattle-heavy employer. If you're making the Seattle → Austin move, here's the honest math — beyond the talking points.

Seattle / Bellevue
  • 🌧️ Average 150+ cloudy/rainy days per year
  • 🏠 Median Bellevue/Eastside home: $1.1M–$1.5M
  • 🏠 3BR comparable to Cedar Park: $1.2M–$1.4M

What You're Getting
Austin, Texas
  • ☀️ 300 days of sunshine per year
  • 🏠 Median Cedar Park home (Bellevue equiv.): $430K–$580K
  • 🏠 That same 3BR: $400K–$550K — not $1.2M
  • 💰 Texas: no income tax (same as Washington)
  • 🚗 Austin traffic: manageable by comparison
  • 🏙️ Amazon Austin: strong but less intense than HQ
  • ☀️ Nov–Mar: 50s–70s, mostly sunny, outdoor life year-round

The tax advantage is smaller coming from Seattle vs. California — both states have no income tax. The real Austin advantage for Seattle Amazonians is housing cost (50–65% less for a comparable home) and 300 days of sunshine. Washington does have a new 7% capital gains tax on gains over $262K — Texas has none.

The Financial Picture

Texas vs. Washington:
The Tax Math for Amazon Employees

💰

Both States: No Income Tax

Washington and Texas both have zero state income tax — so unlike California transplants, Seattle Amazonians don't get a direct income tax savings on W-2 wages. But don't stop there. The total financial picture still favors Austin dramatically when you factor in housing costs and Washington's new capital gains tax.

🏠

Property Tax Reality

Texas property taxes run 1.8%–2.4% — higher than Washington (~1%). On a $500K Cedar Park home, budget $9,000–$12,000/year. But that home would be $1.3M in Bellevue at $14,000–$16,000/year. The absolute tax bill is similar; the home price is 60% less.

📋

Amazon RSU Taxation in Texas

Amazon RSUs vest as ordinary income — both states treat this the same. However, Washington's new 7% capital gains tax on gains over $262K applies to certain equity events. Texas has zero capital gains tax at the state level. For principal engineers and directors with significant equity, this matters.

Before You Arrive

Austin Relocation
Checklist for Amazon Employees

  • Get Texas driver's license within 90 days of establishing residency
  • Register vehicle in Texas (no annual inspection required post-2025)
  • Register to vote in Travis or Williamson County depending on where you live
  • Open a local bank account — Frost Bank, Amplify CU, or UFCU are Austin staples
  • Set up Austin Energy or Pedernales Electric depending on your address
  • File homestead exemption after Jan 1 of first full year of ownership
  • Get pre-approved for a mortgage before your Amazon start date if buying
  • Research school district boundaries — Cedar Park/Leander lines are complex
  • Download TxTag app if commuting via 183A toll road (Cedar Park/Leander route)
  • Budget for Austin summers: AC runs May–October, $180–$280/mo electric

Common Questions

Amazon Austin
Relocation FAQ

Where do most Amazon Austin employees live?

The northwest Austin corridor dominates: Cedar Park and Leander for families (Leander ISD + fast 183A commute), Great Hills and Balcones for established-neighborhood buyers, Domain/Arboretum for singles and couples. Central Austin attracts senior employees with premium budgets. Unlike Amazon Seattle where employees live in Capitol Hill or Ballard, Austin Amazon employees skew suburban — the cost difference makes homeownership too compelling.

How does Austin compare to Seattle for Amazon employees?

Housing is 50–65% cheaper for comparable homes. Both states have no income tax — but Washington's new capital gains tax means Texas can be better for senior employees with large RSU vests. The climate comparison is dramatic: 300 days of Austin sunshine vs. Seattle's 150+. Traffic is better in Austin. The cultural vibe is different — Austin leans outdoor, live music, BBQ; Seattle leans outdoor, coffee, tech-first.

How long is the commute from Austin suburbs to Amazon's offices?

Domain/Arboretum: 5–12 min. Great Hills: 8–15 min. Cedar Park: 15–22 min via 183A. Leander: 20–30 min via 183A. Central Austin/Zilker: 15–25 min via MoPac south. Lakeway/Bee Cave: 25–38 min. Round Rock: 22–35 min via 183 north. Avoid South Austin — wrong direction, 40–60 min against PM traffic.

Does Amazon offer relocation assistance for Austin?

Amazon offers relocation packages for qualifying hires and transfers. For new hires, packages typically cover moving costs with a lump sum or managed move. For transfers, packages tend to be more comprehensive. Confirm your package specifics with Amazon HR — if you have temporary housing covered, use that time to rent in Cedar Park or the Domain and experience the corridor before buying.

Should I rent or buy when relocating from Seattle to Austin for Amazon?

Most Seattle Amazonians I work with move faster on buying than first-time Austin arrivals — they've already done the Bellevue vs. Cedar Park math and the decision is obvious. Renting 6 months is smart if you genuinely don't know Austin; buying immediately makes sense if you have a family, have researched the school districts, and are committed to the move.

Are the schools in Austin as good as Bellevue or Redmond?

Comparable and in some cases better. Leander ISD (Cedar Park, Leander) is consistently ranked among Texas's top large districts — similar caliber to Bellevue School District. Lake Travis ISD (Lakeway) and Eanes ISD (Westlake) rival any in the Pacific Northwest. The key difference: Texas districts have more variability within a single district, so address-specific research matters more than in the uniformly high-performing Bellevue area.
