Why Luke for the Pedernales

The Pedernales is a different market.
It needs a different agent.

Most Austin agents will tell you they "service the Hill Country." Some of them will hand you a Zillow printout, drive you out to a property they've never seen, and try to write a contract on the spot. That works for tract homes in Bee Cave. It does not work along the Pedernales.

The questions that actually swing value here are local-specialist questions. How much usable river frontage does the parcel have? Is the swimming hole spring-fed or seasonal? What's the FEMA floodplain footprint, and does the building envelope sit above it? Is the well good for 8 GPM or 1.5? Is the septic system compliant or grandfathered? Does the property carry an ag or wildlife exemption, and what does that mean for the property tax bill if you change use? These are not questions a generalist Austin agent has answers for.

I represent buyers and sellers across the full developable Pedernales corridor: Spicewood and Briarcliff (the closest stretch to Austin), the Bee Cave Pedernales arm, Johnson City and the Highway 290 corridor, and Stonewall through the LBJ Ranch area. Four counties, four sets of building codes, four different ag-exemption playbooks. I know the corridor, I walk these properties before showing them, and I have a working list of owners who will quietly sell at the right number before they ever hit MLS.

What I Do for Buyers

Buying along the Pedernales.

Map your buying box

We start with a real conversation about what you actually need: full-time vs weekend, target acreage, school requirements, distance from Austin, river-frontage vs view-only, ranch infrastructure, well and septic comfort. Most buyers walk in thinking they want one thing and end up buying something noticeably different. The diligence comes from talking through tradeoffs honestly before we look at any property.

Walk the candidates with you

I personally walk every property we consider. Drone footage and listing photos cannot show you whether the river runs in late August, whether the septic field has standing water, or whether the well casing is original 1970s steel. Walking matters here in a way it does not for tract homes.

Run real diligence

FEMA floodplain analysis, ag and wildlife exemption review, county building code check, septic permit history, well capacity testing, deed restriction pull, mineral and water rights review. I run all of this before you offer, not during option period. Surprises during option are why buyers walk away from properties they would have bought.

Negotiate to your net

Pricing on Hill Country acreage is more art than science. List prices vary widely from market value. I negotiate to your bottom line, not the listing agent's. Concessions on survey, septic curative, well work, and closing-cost allocation often move the deal more than headline price.

What I Do for Sellers

Listing your Pedernales property.

Price to the actual buyer pool

Most Pedernales buyers are out-of-market. They are in Austin, Houston, Dallas, and beyond. They shop on weekend visits. They are sophisticated about river frontage, ranch infrastructure, and Hill Country tradeoffs. Pricing to them, not to a generic Travis County comp sheet, is the difference between a 30-day sale and a 9-month sit.

Photography that shows the asset

Drone footage of the river frontage. Golden-hour shots from the ridgeline. Photography that captures swimming holes, limestone outcroppings, and the actual feel of the property. Phone photography sells nothing on a $1.5M+ Hill Country listing.

Reach buyers across Texas

A Pedernales listing on Central Texas MLS reaches the local pool. The actual buyer pool is across the state - Austin tech, Houston energy, Dallas finance. Targeted digital marketing into those audiences plus referral activation across my professional network is the difference between three weak offers and competitive bidding.

Quiet sales when that matters

Some Pedernales sellers prefer not to publicly list. I run discreet pocket-listing strategies for sellers who value privacy or have specific buyer profiles in mind. The tradeoff is reach, and we will be honest about it before deciding which path makes sense.

The questions that actually swing value here - frontage length, swimming-hole quality, FEMA floodplain, well capacity, septic compliance, ag exemption status, deed restrictions, and which sellers are quietly considering - are local-specialist territory. Working with someone who knows the river is the difference between a good purchase and an avoidable mistake.
The full developable corridor: Spicewood and Briarcliff (Travis and Burnet counties, closest to Austin), Bee Cave-area Pedernales arm, Johnson City and the Highway 290 corridor (Blanco County), and Stonewall through the LBJ Ranch area (Gillespie County). All four counties have different building codes, septic requirements, and ag exemption rules.
