For Families

Austin Schools
for Minneapolis Families

Twin Cities Context

Minneapolis Public Schools and St. Paul Public Schools are highly variable — Edina Public Schools is the suburban gold standard. Families in Uptown or Linden Hills often rely on specific MPLS magnet programs. Austin has the same dynamic: AISD is variable, research by campus address is essential, and the suburban districts (Eanes, Round Rock, Leander) are more consistent.

Austin ISD — Central

AISD's strongest campuses are in Hyde Park, Rosedale, South Austin, and the Zilker/Barton Hills zones. Strong elementaries: Bryker Woods, Casis, Barton Hills, Travis Heights. Strong high schools: McCallum (Hyde Park) and Bowie (South). District-level ratings don't tell the story — research specific campus feeder patterns.

Eanes ISD — Westlake

The Austin equivalent of Edina Public Schools in reputation and consistency — and arguably stronger. Westlake HS is in Texas's top 5. Located in Westlake Hills, 15–20 min from downtown. Homes $900K–$2M+. For Edina and Southwest suburb families who expect Edina-caliber public schools, this is the right answer. Worth the Westlake price premium.

Round Rock / Leander ISD

For Domain-area tech employees, Round Rock ISD and Leander ISD are the right trade: consistently strong public schools in suburban Cedar Park and Round Rock, with more home per dollar than Westlake. Comparable to Eden Prairie or Minnetonka school districts in the Twin Cities. The NW suburb playbook for Austin.

Common Questions

Minneapolis to Austin
FAQ

How much do I save on taxes moving from Minneapolis to Austin?

Minnesota's top income tax rate is 9.85% on income over ~$183K — the second-highest of any state with significant Austin relocation traffic. At $200K income, most Minnesota professionals pay ~8% effective rate, saving ~$16,000/year. At $350K: ~$32,000/year. At $550K+: ~$52,000/year. These savings are permanent, immediate, and apply to all income types including capital gains and RSU vests.

How cold are Minneapolis winters compared to Austin?

Minneapolis averages a 23°F January high and 7°F overnight low, with 54 inches of annual snowfall. Polar vortex events push wind chills to -30°F to -50°F, multiple times per winter. Austin averages a 61°F January high with rare freezing days. Austin gets ~300 sunny days/year; Minneapolis gets ~189. The difference isn't just temperature — it's a fundamentally different relationship with outdoor life from November through March.

What Austin neighborhood is most like Minneapolis's Uptown or North Loop?

North Loop / Warehouse District maps closely to East Austin — converted industrial spaces alongside bungalows, walkable bar and restaurant scene, arts-forward energy. Uptown translates to Bouldin Creek and South Congress — hip, walkable, locally-owned everything, the neighborhood that feels most like Uptown in South Austin form. Linden Hills and Kenwood families typically land in Hyde Park and Rosedale.

How does Minneapolis housing compare to Austin?

Minneapolis is more affordable than most of our other relocation corridors. Uptown and Linden Hills 3BR homes run $500K–$900K; Edina runs $600K–$1.1M. Austin's central neighborhoods are in the same range — Hyde Park and East Austin at $550K–$900K. The housing price comparison isn't the primary case for this move. The 9.85% income tax and the winters are the story.

Can I move to Austin and keep working remotely for a Minneapolis employer?

Yes — and this is the most common pattern for this corridor. Austin is in the Central time zone (same as Minneapolis), making remote work for Twin Cities employers completely seamless. You capture the full 9.85% Minnesota income tax savings the moment you establish Texas residency. Confirm with your employer that state tax withholding changes to Texas, and file a Minnesota part-year return for your move year.

How are Austin schools for Minneapolis families?

AISD and Minneapolis Public Schools have similar campus-level variability — strong in specific neighborhoods, inconsistent elsewhere. Edina Public Schools in the Twin Cities maps to Eanes ISD (Westlake Hills) in Austin — one of Texas's top districts with Westlake HS in the state's top 5. AISD's best campuses are in Hyde Park, South Austin, and Zilker zones. Round Rock ISD and Leander ISD serve the NW suburb corridor comparably to Eden Prairie and Minnetonka school districts.
