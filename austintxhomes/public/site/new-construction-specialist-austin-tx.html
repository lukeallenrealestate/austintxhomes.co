Why it matters

The Model Home Sales Agent Isn't Your Friend.

The smiling person at the model home works for the builder — their job is maximizing the builder's margin, not yours. When you walk in without your own representation, you lose the strongest leverage in the transaction. Here's what a new construction specialist actually does.

💰

Price + Upgrade Negotiation

Builders rarely lower base price (it hurts comps), but will negotiate on closing costs, design center upgrades, appliance packages, rate buy-downs. I know which levers to pull and when — end of month, end of quarter, standing inventory vs build-to-order.

📋

Contract Review

Builder contracts are written for the builder. Liquidated damages clauses, change-order fees, warranty carve-outs, earnest money forfeiture — there's a lot in there that matters. I flag the worst clauses and negotiate revisions before you sign.

🏠

Lot Selection

The lot matters more than the house. Drainage, trees, sun exposure, neighbors, traffic patterns, future development. I've walked these communities through the build-out cycle and know which lots to fight for and which to avoid.

🔧

Upgrade Strategy

Builders upcharge 40-200% on design center upgrades. Some are worth it (structural changes, premium flooring you can't retrofit). Most aren't (appliances, paint, fixtures). I'll show you which upgrades return equity and which ones burn cash.

🔍

Third-Party Inspections

New doesn't mean perfect. I coordinate pre-drywall, pre-closing, and 11-month warranty inspections with independent inspectors — catching defects while the builder's still on the hook to fix them. This saves clients $10K–$50K in future repairs on average.

🤝

Free to You

Builders pay my commission (2.5-3%). My fee comes out of the transaction whether you bring me or not — you literally pay the same price, you just get full representation and expert guidance by having me there. Walking in unrepresented leaves money and leverage on the table.

Common questions

New Construction FAQ

Do I need a realtor for new construction in Austin?+
Yes — absolutely. The onsite sales agent at a builder's model home represents the builder, not you. Buyer representation is free (builders pay the commission) and gets you honest advice on pricing, upgrades, builder reputation, and contract negotiation. Walking in without representation is one of the biggest mistakes buyers make.
Which are the best new construction builders in Austin?+
Top builders: KB Home, Meritage, Taylor Morrison, Toll Brothers, Drees, Highland, Lennar, David Weekley, Pulte, Coventry, Milestone. Each has different strengths — build quality, design flexibility, warranty programs. I've worked with all of them and can tell you exactly which ones perform well in your target neighborhood.
Can I negotiate with a new construction builder?+
Yes, but differently. Builders rarely lower base price (hurts comps), but they will negotiate on: closing cost credits ($5K-$20K), design center upgrades, appliance packages, fence and landscaping, rate buy-downs. Knowing which levers to pull and when is where an experienced specialist earns their keep.
How much is the builder rebate for buying new construction?+
Builder commissions typically run 2.5-3% to the buyer's agent. Some agents share a "rebate" with the buyer, but saving $5K on commission to lose $25K on poor upgrade choices is a bad trade. I don't discount commission because experience pays for itself many times over.
What are the best new construction communities in 2026?+
Hot communities: Easton Park, Santa Rita Ranch, Sweetwater, Rancho Sienna, Goodnight Ranch, Mueller, Whisper Valley, Headwaters. Each has different price points, lot sizes, and target buyer profiles. Contact me for a specific recommendation based on your needs.
How long does new construction take in Austin?+
5-10 months from contract to closing. Production builders: 4-6 months on standing inventory, 6-8 months build-to-order. Custom/semi-custom: 8-12 months. Weather, labor, and design changes can extend by 30-60 days.
