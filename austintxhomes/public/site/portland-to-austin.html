Oregon's Layered Tax Burden

Oregon's 9.9% State + Local
Tax Stack

Oregon's top marginal income tax rate is 9.9% — the third-highest in the US. But for Portland residents, it doesn't stop there. Multnomah County added a 1.5% income tax in 2021. The Metro Supportive Housing Services district added another 1% in 2021. For a Portland professional earning $200K+, the effective combined rate on income over the thresholds is 12.4%. Texas has no state income tax, no county income tax, and no city income tax. Zero, stacked.

Portland has genuinely exceptional outdoor access — Forest Park alone is more urban greenspace than most US cities will ever have. The Columbia River Gorge and Mt. Hood are world-class. The trade isn't about outdoor quality — it's about outdoor seasonality. Portland's outdoor culture runs roughly May through October. Austin's outdoor culture runs twelve months: January kayaking on Lady Bird Lake, March swimming at Barton Springs, July morning runs on the Greenbelt at 6am. The sun difference (144 days vs. 300 days) is the most commonly cited factor by Portland transplants — often underestimated until the first Austin February.

On Portland culture: Portland's food cart culture has a direct Austin equivalent — Austin's food trailer scene is one of the best in the US. Powell's Books has no Austin equivalent (BookPeople is beloved but not Powell's). The craft beer trade is close — Austin's brewery scene is deep. Austin is the most Portland-compatible Texas city by a wide margin: both are music cities, both skew progressive, both have strong cycling and outdoor communities.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked
Questions

How much is Oregon's income tax and what's the Texas savings?

Oregon's top state income tax rate is 9.9% (on income over $125K single). Portland residents also pay Multnomah County's 1.5% and the Metro SHS 1% — bringing the combined top rate to 12.4% for Portland proper. Texas has no state income tax. For a Portland resident earning $250K, the annual combined OR/county/metro tax bill is approximately $30,000. In Texas: $0. Over 10 years, that's $300,000 in tax savings.

Does Oregon try to tax me after I move to Texas?

Oregon taxes income earned by Oregon residents. Moving to Texas and establishing Texas domicile ends Oregon's claim on your income going forward. Unlike California, Oregon doesn't have the same aggressive audit reputation for movers — but high-income earners with significant unvested RSUs or business income should consult a CPA on domicile timing relative to income events.

Where do Intel Hillsboro employees typically land in Austin?

Intel's Austin presence is in the NW Austin/Domain corridor — the same tech campus area as Apple, Google, Meta, and Nvidia. Intel Hillsboro transplants typically land in Domain-adjacent neighborhoods: Avery Ranch, NW Austin, Cedar Park, or Round Rock. For families prioritizing schools, Westlake Hills (Eanes ISD) is 25–30 minutes from the Domain and offers comparable school quality to Lake Oswego SD.

Is Austin compatible with Portland's culture?

More than people expect. Austin and Portland share food cart culture (Austin's trailer parks are a direct descendant), strong live music culture (different genres), progressive politics in the city core, outdoor-oriented lifestyles, and cycling communities. Austin is more car-dependent outside the core, statewide politics are more conservative, and the terrain is limestone canyon instead of Pacific NW forest. Most Portland transplants find Austin's pace and outdoor year-round access make the adjustment smooth.

How does Austin housing compare to Portland?

Central Portland and central Austin are remarkably similar in price: Alberta Arts and East Austin, Hawthorne and South Congress, Pearl District and the Domain all overlap in the $500K–$900K range. Portland's property tax (~1.0–1.1%) is lower than Austin's (1.8–2.4%), partially offsetting income tax savings at lower price points. The full financial advantage of Austin is primarily the income tax stack — at $250K income, that's $22,500–$30,000 per year depending on where in Portland you live.

What's Austin's outdoor scene like compared to Portland?

Portland's outdoor access (Forest Park, Gorge, Mt. Hood) is genuinely exceptional — Austin can't match Pacific NW scenery for sheer drama. What Austin has instead: year-round access. Barton Springs Pool is swimmable in January. The Greenbelt is hikeable every month. Lady Bird Lake is kayakable in February. Portland's outdoor culture is excellent but seasonal — roughly 5 good months. Austin's is twelve months.
