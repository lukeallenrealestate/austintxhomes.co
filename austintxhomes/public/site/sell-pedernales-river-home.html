Why pricing is different here

Selling Pedernales does not work like selling Austin.

Most listing strategies that work in urban Austin fail along the Pedernales. The reasons are structural: your buyer is mostly out-of-market. They live in Austin, Houston, Dallas, and beyond. They shop on weekend visits. They are sophisticated about river frontage, ranch infrastructure, and Hill Country tradeoffs. They expect to negotiate, and they expect a listing agent who knows the difference between premium frontage and seasonal trickle.

Pricing for a buyer pool you cannot see in person every day means pricing aggressively but not punitively. Listings that start 15 to 25 percent above current comps based on 2022 peak data do not sell. They age, take price cuts, and net the seller less than a properly-priced listing would have. The most expensive mistake in Pedernales listing strategy is over-pricing on day one.

The second most expensive mistake is under-marketing. A listing that only shows up on Central Texas MLS reaches the local pool, which is roughly a third of the actual buyer demand. The other two-thirds are reading luxury Hill Country newsletters in Austin, Houston, and Dallas, browsing waterfront aggregator sites, and asking their network for Pedernales referrals.

The Listing Process

How Luke sells a Pedernales property.

01

Pricing strategy

Detailed comparative analysis controlling for river frontage quality, acreage, ranch infrastructure, school district, and county tax basis. Pricing is set to the actual buyer pool, not last quarter's comps or 2022 peak prints.

02

Pre-list preparation

Specific recommendations for what is worth fixing before listing and what is not. Most "renovate before listing" advice from generalist agents costs Pedernales sellers money. Selective updates with measurable ROI is the bar.

03

Professional media

Drone work showing river frontage and acreage context. Twilight photography. Lake Travis ISD attendance zone visualization where applicable. Interior photography matched to the property's actual buyer profile.

04

Out-of-market reach

Targeted digital marketing into Austin, Houston, Dallas, and San Antonio Hill Country and ranch buyer audiences. Luxury real estate newsletter placements. Referral activation across my professional network.

05

Showing logistics

Out-of-market buyers schedule weekend trips and want to tour multiple properties in clusters. I coordinate showing schedules to maximize the small windows when serious buyers are physically present.

06

Off-market option

Some sellers prefer not to publicly list, especially for legacy ranch properties or privacy-sensitive sales. I run discreet pocket-listing strategies when that is the right call. The tradeoff is reach, and we will be honest about it before deciding which path makes sense.

Median days on market on the Pedernales runs 60 to 120 days. Well-priced river-frontage listings with strong photography can sell in 30 to 60 days. Off-river inventory or homes priced above market can sit 9 to 18 months. Spring through early fall is the strongest selling season, when out-of-market buyers actively shop weekend visits during good weather.
Texas listing commissions are negotiable. Market standard for full-service listings runs 5 to 6 percent total (split between listing agent and buyer agent). For higher-priced Pedernales properties (typically $2M+), some sellers negotiate to lower percentages on the listing side because absolute dollar commissions are still meaningful at high price points. The mix varies by submarket - Spicewood skews more Austin-local while Stonewall and Johnson City draw a higher share of weekend buyers from Houston and Dallas.

Pedernales pricing is more nuanced than urban Austin.
